Transition Monotones and the Lyapunov Function of the Cosmos

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We identify the "C-theorem" of classical and quantum dynamics. We propose a universal Lyapunov function $\mathcal{L}$ governing all regime transitions. We show that the Renormalization Group flow, the H-theorem, and the Area Law are all special cases of the monotonicity of $\mathcal{L}$. Theoretical biology and cosmology are thus unified under a single optimization principle.

The universe doesn't just change; it descends. We define the height function.

The Generalized C-Theorem

$$ \frac{d\mathcal{L}}{dt} \le 0 $$

$\mathcal{L}$ is a measure of the "Distance from Equilibrium". In QFT, $\mathcal{L}$ corresponds to the central charge $c$ (Zamolodchikov C-theorem). In Thermodynamics, $\mathcal{L} = -S$. In GR, $\mathcal{L}$ is the inverse of the horizon area.
