Applications of Tamesis Theory: Topological Encryption and Computing

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Beyond its implications for fundamental physics, the Tamesis framework offers novel algorithms for information processing. We demonstrate that the $U_{1/2}$ universality class allows for a new type of Error Correction Code (ECC) based on the topology of prime geodesics. We propose "Riemannian Cryptography," a scheme secured not by factoring difficulty but by the chaotic scattering of information on hyperbolic manifolds.

Theoretical physics often spawns technology (Quantum Computing, GPS). Tamesis, being a theory of information geometry, naturally suggests applications in computation.

1. Topological Error Correction

Current Quantum Error Correction (Surface Code) relies on topological invariants of a torus (genus 1). Tamesis suggests using a genus $g \ge 2$ manifold (Hyperbolic Surface).

Application: Hyperbolic Surface Codes
Using a lattice connectivity based on the $\{7,3\}$ tiling of the Poincaré disk, bit-flip errors require macroscopic energy to propagate, suppressing error rates exponentially faster than square lattices.

2. Riemannian Cryptography

RSA encryption relies on the difficulty of finding prime factors. We propose a physical encryption scheme based on the Inverse Spectral Problem.

Proposed Protocol: Encode the message into the shape of a drum (manifold). Transmit the eigenvalues (sound). The receiver reconstructs the shape. An eavesdropper must solve the Inverse Selberg Trace Formula, which is computationally equivalent to finding zeros of the Zeta function—a problem believed to be strictly harder than factoring.

Conclusion

The mathematics of Tamesis—Spectral Geometry and Number Theory—are the native language of optimal information theory. The universe computes efficiently; we should copy its architecture.
