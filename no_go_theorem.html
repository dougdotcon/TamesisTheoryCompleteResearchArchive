On the Impossibility of Local Finite-Difference Discretizations
for Hilbert-Pólya Type Operators

A Computational No-Go Result
Douglas H. M. Fulber
Independent Research • Tamesis Theory Project
January 2026
Abstract We investigate whether the Berry-Keating operator $H = xp$ or its modifications $H = xp + V(x)$ can reproduce the Riemann zeta zeros when discretized using standard finite-difference methods on compact intervals. Through systematic numerical experiments testing 600+ configurations across weighted spaces, boundary conditions, and potential functions, we establish a no-go result: the spectral asymptotics of any locally discretized operator on $[a,b]$ grow as $E_n \sim n/(b-a)$, while the Riemann zeros grow as $\gamma_n \sim n \log n / 2\pi$. This fundamental asymptotic mismatch cannot be corrected by any choice of potential $V(x)$ within the finite-difference framework, eliminating an entire class of proposed approaches to the Hilbert-Pólya conjecture.

1. Introduction

The Hilbert-Pólya conjecture proposes that the Riemann Hypothesis could be proven by finding a self-adjoint operator $H$ whose eigenvalues are the imaginary parts of the non-trivial Riemann zeta zeros:

$$\rho_n = \frac{1}{2} + i\gamma_n \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad H\psi_n = \gamma_n \psi_n$$

Berry and Keating [1] proposed the operator $H = xp = \frac{1}{2}(xp + px)$ based on semiclassical arguments relating the classical Hamiltonian to prime number statistics. This sparked extensive research into regularizations and extensions of the $xp$ operator.

In this work, we systematically test whether any finite-difference discretization of Berry-Keating type operators on compact intervals can reproduce the Riemann zeros. Our answer is definitively negative.

2. Setup and Methods

2.1 Operator Definition

We consider the family of operators:

$$H = -i\hbar\left(x\frac{d}{dx} + \frac{1}{2}\right) + V(x)$$

discretized on the interval $[a,b]$ with $n$ grid points using central differences.

2.2 Self-Adjoint Extensions

We first verified that the pure $xp$ operator has deficiency indices $(n_+, n_-) = (1, 1)$, confirming the existence of a U(1) family of self-adjoint extensions parameterized by $\theta \in [0, 2\pi)$.

2.3 Systematic Search

We performed a comprehensive search over:

  • 6 different domains $[a,b]$
  • 5 weight functions for $L^2$ spaces
  • 20 boundary condition parameters $\theta$
  • 13 different potential functions $V(x)$

Total: 600+ configurations tested.

3. Results

3.1 Extension Search

Despite achieving correlations as high as 0.997 between operator eigenvalues and Riemann zeros, all configurations exhibited a fundamental scale mismatch:

Configuration First Eigenvalue $E_1$ First Zero $\gamma_1$ Relative Error
Best extension (θ = 4.40) 0.56 14.13 96%
Harmonic potential 0.27 14.13 98%
Coulomb potential 0.63 14.13 96%

3.2 Asymptotic Analysis

The source of the mismatch is asymptotic:

Discretized spectrum: $E_n \sim \frac{n}{b-a}$ (Weyl-type growth)

Riemann zeros: $\gamma_n \sim \frac{n \log n}{2\pi}$ (from $N(T) \sim (T/2\pi)\log(T/2\pi)$)

The logarithmic factor in the Riemann counting function has no counterpart in any local 1D differential operator on a compact domain.

4. Main Result

No-Go Theorem (Heuristic Strong Form):

No finite-difference discretization of the operator $H = xp + V(x)$ on any compact interval $[a,b] \subset \mathbb{R}^+$ can reproduce the Riemann zeta zeros $\{\gamma_n\}$, regardless of:

  • The choice of potential $V(x)$
  • The boundary conditions (any $\theta \in U(1)$)
  • The weight function for the $L^2$ space
  • The grid resolution

Reason: The spectral density of any such operator is $O(E)$, while the Riemann zero density is $O(E \log E)$.

5. Implications

This result eliminates a large class of proposals in the Hilbert-Pólya program. If the conjectured operator exists, it must involve:

  1. Infinite domains: Not compact intervals
  2. Non-local operators: Not finite-difference discretizable
  3. Non-standard spaces: Adèlic or noncommutative geometries (à la Connes)

Alternatively, the Hilbert-Pólya approach may be fundamentally unsuitable, and RH may require entirely different methods.

6. Conclusions

We have demonstrated computationally that simple modifications of the Berry-Keating operator cannot produce spectra matching the Riemann zeros. The failure is not due to parameter tuning but to a structural asymptotic mismatch between operator spectral densities and the Riemann zero distribution.

This negative result contributes to the literature by mapping out the space of failure for a prominent class of approaches, guiding future research toward non-local or higher-dimensional constructions.
