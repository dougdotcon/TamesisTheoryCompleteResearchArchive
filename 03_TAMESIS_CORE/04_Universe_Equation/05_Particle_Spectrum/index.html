Topological Origin of the Particle Spectrum

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We address the "Generation Problem" of the Standard Model by modeling elementary particles as topological solitons (knots) in the spacetime vacuum field. We demonstrate that the three generations of quarks correspond to the three simplest prime knots: Trefoil ($3_1$), Figure-Eight ($4_1$), and Cinquefoil ($5_1$). Their masses scale exponentially with the knot's ideal rope length ($L/D$), providing a first-principles derivation of the mass hierarchy.

1. The Geometry of Mass

In the Tamesis framework, mass is not an intrinsic property but the energy cost of maintaining a topological defect. The more complex the knot, the more energy required to stabilize it against the vacuum tension (dark energy).

Mass-Topology Relation
$$M \approx M_0 \exp\left(\alpha \cdot \frac{L}{D}\right)$$

Where $L/D$ is the ideal rope length of the knot, a fundamental topological invariant.

3. Mass Scaling Law

Plotting the observed quark masses against the ideal rope length reveals a striking exponential relationship.

Up-Sector Scaling:
The mass jump from Up to Charm to Top follows an exponential growth driven by knot complexity. $$M_u \propto e^{1.53 (L/D)}$$

Adding a single crossing (increasing $N$ by 1) increases the curvature energy by factor of $\sim 90-100$, explaining why the Top quark is so unexpectedly heavy.

4. Origin of Spin-1/2

Why do fermions have spin-1/2? In this framework, fermions are Moebius topologies. An object connected to the spacetime metric via a "twisted ribbon" (wormhole throat) must be rotated by 720 degrees (4$\pi$) to return to its original configuration.

$$\Psi(\theta + 2\pi) = -\Psi(\theta)$$ $$\Psi(\theta + 4\pi) = +\Psi(\theta)$$

5. Conclusion

The Standard Model parameters are not arbitrary. They are determined by the discrete library of allowed topological knots in 3+1 spacetime. The "Generation Problem" is solved: configurations with $N>5$ crossings are likely unstable (too high energy), limiting the universe to three stable generations.
