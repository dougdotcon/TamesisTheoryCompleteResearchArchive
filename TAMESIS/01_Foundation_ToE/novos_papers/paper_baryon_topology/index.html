Baryon Topology: Protons as Braided Knots

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Abstract. Protons contain three quarks (uud) with a total current mass of ~9 MeV, yet the proton mass is 938 MeV. Where does 99% of the mass come from? The standard answer relies on QCD binding energy. Here, we present the TAMESIS interpretation: baryons are braided knots. We propose that the extra mass arises from the topological complexity (crossing number) of the gluonic braids connecting the quarks. This topological model provides a geometric origin for confinement and mass generation.

1. The Mass Puzzle

The discrepancy between constituent quark masses and the total baryon mass is striking:

$$m_{uud} = 2(2.2) + 4.7 = 9.1 \text{ MeV}$$ $$m_{proton} = 938.3 \text{ MeV}$$ $$\text{Binding} \approx 99\% \text{ of mass}$$

2. Braided Knots

In the TAMESIS framework, quarks are modeled as trefoil knots (3 crossings each). A baryon is not merely a collection of 3 separate knots, but a braided structure where gluons act as the braiding strings. We postulate that these braids add approximately 100 effective crossings to the system, which manifests as the 'binding energy' mass.

3. Conclusion

Binding Energy = Braiding Complexity
QCD confinement is identified as a topological necessity in TAMESIS.
