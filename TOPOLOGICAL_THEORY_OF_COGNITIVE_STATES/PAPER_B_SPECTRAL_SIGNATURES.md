# Spectral Signatures of Depressive Regimes: A Topo-Dynamic Reinterpretation

**Douglas H. M. Fulber**
*Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro*

---

## Abstract

We propose a theoretical reinterpretation of major psychiatric disorders not as chemical imbalances, but as specific failure modes of the brain's top-dynamic regime. By mapping Neural Oscillations (EEG) to the Eigenvalues of the Connectome Laplacian (Spectral Graph Theory), we identify distinct "Spectral Signatures" for depression and anxiety. We argue that **Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)** corresponds to a **Spectral Red-Shift** (dominance of low-frequency/high-damping modes) caused by topological disconnectedness (The Entropic Trap) or increased medium viscosity. Conversely, **Generalized Anxiety** corresponds to a **Spectral Blue-Shift** and topological overfitting. This framework unifies structural and functional findings under a single physics-based diagnostic criteria.

## 1. Introduction: The Limits of Chemical Reductionism

The monoamine hypothesis of depression has reached an explanatory plateau. While neurotransmitters (Serotonin, Dopamine) undoubtedly play a role, their function is akin to the "oil" in an engine, whereas the failure often lies in the "mechanical geometry" of the pistons.

We propose a shift from **Neurochemistry** to **Neurotopology**.
If the mind is the traffic on a network (Postulate 1 of Relational Cognition), then pathology is a traffic jam, not just a fuel shortage.

## 2. Theoretical Bridge: From Graph to Spectrum

In *Topological Theory of Cognitive States* (Paper A), we established that the state of a cognitive system is defined by the spectrum of its Laplacian matrix $L$.
Here, we map this formal math to biological observables.

### 2.1 The Rosetta Stone of Neuro-Spectral Analysis

| Graph Theoretical Entity (Model) | Neurophysiological Correlate (Measurable) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Laplacian Eigenvalues ($\lambda$)** | **Neural Oscillation Frequencies (EEG Bands)** |
| Low $\lambda$ (Global Modes) | Delta ($\delta$) / Theta ($\theta$) Bands |
| High $\lambda$ (Local Modes) | Beta ($\beta$) / Gamma ($\gamma$) Bands |
| **Algebraic Connectivity ($\lambda_2$)** | **Global Phase Synchrony / Integration** |
| **Damping Coefficient ($\zeta$)** | **Neural Variability / Signal-to-Noise Ratio** |

This mapping allows us to treat an EEG readout not just as "activity", but as the **acoustic signature of the connectome's geometry**.

## 3. The Spectral Signatures of Pathology

We identify three primary pathological regimes based on their spectral deformation.

### 3.1 The "Entropic Trap" (The Depressive Signature)

**Phenomenology:** Anhedonia, psychomotor retardation, "stuck" thoughts, rumination.

**Topological Cause:**
The network forms a deep **local attractor** with high energy barriers ($\Delta E \gg kT$). The topology is rigid (low plasticity).

**Spectral Signature (The Red Shift):**

1. **Collapse of High Frequencies:** Diminished Gamma activity (loss of local binding/vividness).
2. **Dominance of Low Eigenvalues:** The spectrum is compressed towards $\lambda \to 0$. The brain gets "bass-heavy".
3. **Low Complexity:** Reduced Lempel-Ziv complexity of the signal.

**Interpretation:** The system lacks the "kinetic energy" (perturbation) to escape the local basin of attraction.

### 3.2 "Oscillatory Chaos" (The Anxious Signature)

**Phenomenology:** Hyper-vigilance, racing thoughts, inability to focus, noise sensitivity.

**Topological Cause:**
**Overfitting**. The network creates excessive short-range loops (high Clustering Coefficient). Feedback loops amplify noise.

**Spectral Signature (The Blue Shift):**

1. **High-Frequency Noise:** Excessive Beta/Gamma power that is *not* phase-locked.
2. **Harmonic Dissonance:** Failure of Cross-Frequency Coupling (Theta-Gamma decoupling).
3. **High Entropy:** But "bad" entropy (randomness), not structural complexity.

### 3.3 "The Viscous Medium" (The Metabolic Signature)

**Phenomenology:** Brain fog, slow processing (Bradyphrenia), treatment-resistant depression.

**Physical Cause:**
This is not a topological defect of the edges ($w_{ij}$), but a property of the medium ($\rho$). High levels of inflammatory markers or cortisol act as a **viscous damper** on signal propagation.

**Spectral Signature (Overdamping):**

1. **Global Amplitude Reduction:** Flattening of the Power Spectral Density (PSD) curve ($1/f^\alpha$, where $\alpha$ increases).
2. **Slow Decay:** Impulse response functions show prolonged decay times.

## 4. Therapeutic Implications (Reframing)

If the diagnosis is topological, the treatment must be geometric.

1. **For Entropic Traps (Depression I):**
    * **Goal:** Injection of Energy / Perturbation ("Shake the box").
    * **Mechanism:** TMS, ECT, or Psychedelics acting as "Simulated Annealing" (increasing system temperature $T$ to jump out of the local minimum).

2. **For Oscillatory Chaos (Anxiety):**
    * **Goal:** Regularization / Damping.
    * **Mechanism:** Meditation (topological pruning), Benzodiazepines (increasing inhibition/damping threshold).

3. **For Viscous Medium (Depression II):**
    * **Goal:** Viscosity Reduction.
    * **Mechanism:** Anti-inflammatory strategies, hormonal regulation. (Boosting signal in a viscous medium is futile; one must thin the medium).

## 5. Conclusion

We conclude that "Depression" is a bucket term for distinct physical failures. By analyzing the **Spectral Signature**, we can distinguish between a system that is "trapped" (needs energy), "chaotic" (needs tuning), or "viscous" (needs cleaning). This reinterpretation opens the door for **Physics-Based Precision Psychiatry**.
