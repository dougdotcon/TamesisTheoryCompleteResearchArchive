The TDTR Simulation Program: Methodology and Scope

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We outline the computational strategy used to validate the Thermodynamic Time Reversal (TDTR) theory. Unlike analytical proofs, which deal with idealized cases, numerical simulations allow us to test the theory in the messy, non-linear regime of the real universe. We describe the "ReactiveCosmoMapper" engine, which simulates the entropic response of spacetime to matter distributions without assuming Dark Matter.

Theory is nothing without a compiler. We compiled the universe to check for bugs.

The Algorithm

The core of our simulation is the Poisson-Boltzmann equation for Entropic Gravity:

$$ \nabla \cdot (\frac{1}{a_0} \nabla \Phi) = 4\pi G \rho $$

This non-linear equation replaces the standard Poisson equation. It accounts for the "stiffness" of spacetime due to information saturation at low accelerations.
