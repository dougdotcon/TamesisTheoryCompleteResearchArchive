The Topological Theory of Cognitive States: Spectral Signatures and Thermodynamic Limits

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 28, 2026)
Abstract> We propose a physics-based framework where "cognitive states" are defined not by semantic content, but by the topological regime of the underlying dynamic network. Building on the Information Saturation Postulate, we demonstrate that attention is a thermodynamic necessity for systems operating near channel capacity. We identify distinct topological regimes—Critical Integration (Healthy), Entropic Trapping (Depression), and Modular Fragmentation (Dissociation)—and map them to specific spectral signatures of the connectome Laplacian.

The quantitative description of cognitive states remains an open problem. We hypothesize that what are perceived as macro-states of mind are phase states of information flow on a dynamic graph.

Part I: The Foundations of Relational Cognition

We adopt a minimal ontology: the system is defined by nodes (processing units) and edges (causal relations), subject to physical constraints.

Postulate 1 (Relational Dynamics):
Cognitive systems are dynamic networks whose evolution is strictly governed by the redistribution of information, subject to physical constraints of capacity and metabolic cost.

Part II: Topological Regimes

We identify three primary classes of network configurations:

  1. Regime I: Critical Integration ("Healthy"). Small-World topology. Maximizes dynamic range at the "Edge of Chaos".
  2. Regime II: Entropic Trapping ("Depression"). Deep attractor basins with high energy barriers. The system is "stuck" in rigid loops (Rumination).
  3. Regime III: Modular Fragmentation ("Dissociation"). Breakdown of the Giant Component. Signals decouple into isolated subgraphs.

Part III: Spectral Signatures of Pathology

By analyzing the eigenvalues of the Connectome Laplacian, we find:

  • Spectral Red-Shift (Depression): Dominance of low-frequency modes ($\lambda \to 0$). Loss of Gamma activity. The brain is "bass-heavy" and rigid.
  • Spectral Blue-Shift (Anxiety): Excessive high-frequency noise. Topological overfitting leads to "Oscillatory Chaos".

Part IV: Therapeutic Implications

If the diagnosis is topological, the treatment must be geometric. Depression requires energy injection (TMS, Psychedelics) to escape local minima ("Simulated Annealing"). Anxiety requires regularization (Meditation, Inhibition) to prune excessive loops.

Conclusion

Cognitive Science is a branch of Statistical Physics. "Consciousness" is the process of topological binding that integrates information into a unified causal structure. Pathology is the failure of this integration due to thermodynamic constraints.
