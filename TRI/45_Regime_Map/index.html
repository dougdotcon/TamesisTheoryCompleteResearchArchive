Stage 45: The Regime Map and the Absence of a Center

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We present a comprehensive map of 14 distinct physical regimes cataloged by their $(S, P, O)$ signatures. Topological analysis of this map reveals a "hole" in the center: there is no single regime that shares invariants with all others. This finding constitutes an empirical proof that physics is structurally decentralized. Instead of a pyramid with a unified Theory of Everything at the apex, physics is a graph of locally valid regimes connected by phase transitions. We identify the specific invariant conflicts that prevent the unification of String Theory, Loop Quantum Gravity, and Holographic dualities with the full range of observed phenomena.

The history of physics is often presented as a path toward unification: from Newton to Maxwell, to Einstein, and finally to the Standard Model. This narrative implies a convergence toward a single pointâ€”a "Theory of Everything" (ToE). TRI challenges this narrative by analyzing the structural compatibility of accepted theories.

In this Letter, we construct the "Regime Map", a topological representation of the space of physical theories. Each node is a regime; each edge is a feasible transition. The connectivity of this graph reveals fundamental obstructions to global unification.

Proof of Decentralization

A Unified Theory $T^*$ must contain all other regimes as limiting cases. This implies that the invariants of $T^*$ must be compatible with the invariants of all sub-regimes. However, we observe mutually exclusive invariants:

1. QFT requires Non-commutative local fields.

2. GR requires Commutative local curvature.

3. Computational dynamics require Discrete counting.

There exists no mathematical structure that is simultaneously Discrete and Continuous, Commutative and Non-commutative. Thus, $T^*$ cannot exist.

Why ToE Attempts Fail

We can categorize failed ToEs by which invariants they violate:

- String Theory: Assumes a continuous background (violates discrete regimes like Ising/Computation).

- Loop Quantum Gravity: Discretizes spacetime (violates continuous invariants of standard GR/QFT).

- Holographic (AdS/CFT): Relies on boundary/bulk duality which is not universal to all geometries (e.g., dS space).

The failure is not one of precision, but of category. You cannot construct a sphere from only flat tiles without gaps.

Conclusion

Physics is a graph, not a pyramid. The fundamental objects of study should shift from the nodes (state laws) to the edges (transition laws). This perspective resolves the conflict between QFT and GR by treating them as distinct nodes connected by an irreversible transition [1], rather than forcing them into a single incompatible framework.
