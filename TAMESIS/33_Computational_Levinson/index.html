Computational Test of Levinson's Theorem on Graphs

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Levinson's Theorem relates the number of bound states in a potential to the scattering phase shift at zero energy. We extend this to discrete graphs. Using the python script `computational_levinson.py`, we numerically verify that the number of bound states on a "defective" lattice corresponds exactly to the winding number of the scattering matrix. This confirms that topological invariants are robust even in discrete, pixelated space.

Bound states are topology. Scattering is geometry. Levinson's theorem says they are the same.

The Discrete Theorem

$$ N_{bound} = \frac{1}{\pi} (\delta(0) - \delta(\infty)) $$

Our code constructs a tight-binding Hamiltonian with a vacancy defect. We diagonalize it to find eigenvalues outside the continuous band ($N_{bound}$) and compute the phase shift of the scattering states. The match is exact.
