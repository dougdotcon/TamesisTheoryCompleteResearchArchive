Fundamental Directionality: Why Nature Has No Reverse Gear

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We provide a topological proof of the irreversibility of regime transitions. By modeling the space of regimes as a directed graph (DAG), we show that loops (cycles) are forbidden by the "Information Loss Lemma." This implies that while laws can evolve, they can never return to a previous state. The universe is not cyclical; it is a one-way trip towards maximum complexity and then maximum entropy.

Time travel is impossible not just technically, but logically. To reverse time, one would need to reconstruct lost information.

The Data Processing Inequality

The fundamental reason for irreversibility is the Data Processing Inequality (DPI):

$$ I(X; Z) \le I(X; Y) $$

If a regime $Y$ emerges from $X$, and $Z$ emerges from $Y$, the mutual information with the origin $X$ strictly decreases. We lose contact with the "Initial Condition" (The Big Bang) the further we evolve.
