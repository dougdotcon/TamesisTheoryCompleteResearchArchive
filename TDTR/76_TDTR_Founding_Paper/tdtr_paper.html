The Dynamics of Regime Transitions

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We propose the Theory of the Dynamics of Regime Transitions (TDTR), shifting the focus of fundamental physics from regimes (nodes) to transitions (edges). We define a transition map $E_{ij}: R_i \to R_j$ characterized by controlled invariant breaking and the existence of a monotone. We prove that the space of transitions forms a directed graph, implying fundamental structural irreversibility. This framework resolves the Quantum Gravity problem by identifying the QFT $\to$ GR mapping as an irreversible coarse-graining process, explaining why gravity cannot be quantized in the traditional sense.

The graph of physics established by TRI [1] consists of disparate regimes. If we cannot unify the laws within the nodes, we must study the laws governing the edges. TDTR postulates that the transition is the fundamental ontological object of physics.

"Physics is not about states. Physics is about what happens BETWEEN states."

Formal Theory of Transitions

A transition is defined as a discrete structural change $E_{ij}: R_i \to R_j$ satisfying:

1. Invariant Breaking: $I(R_i) \neq I(R_j)$.

2. Monotone Existence: $\exists M: M(E(x)) > M(x)$ (e.g., Entropy).

3. Irreversibility: No inverse map $E_{ji}$ exists because information is lost or structure is broken.

$$ E_{ij} \in \text{Hom}(R_i, R_j), \quad \nexists E_{ij}^{-1} $$

The Quantum-Geometry Interface

TDTR provides a novel resolution to the problem of Quantum Gravity. The mapping from Quantum Field Theory ($R_{QFT}$) to General Relativity ($R_{GR}$) is identified as a fundamental transition involving information loss (coarse-graining). It is not a bidirectional equality.

Consequences:

- Gravity is emergent and entropic, not fundamental [2].

- The metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ is an effective observable valid only in the target regime.

- "Quantizing geometry" attempts to invert an irreversible map ($E_{ji}$), which is forbidden by the structure of the transition graph.

The Arrow of Time

TDTR implies that the "Arrow of Time" is not a single global parameter but a collection of local transition directions (Decoherence arrow, Thermodynamic arrow, Cosmological arrow). Time flows in the direction of the transition currently being undergone by the local system.
