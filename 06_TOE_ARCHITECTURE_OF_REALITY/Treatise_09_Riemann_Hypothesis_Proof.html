The Thermodynamic Proof of the Riemann Hypothesis: Spectral Stability at the Critical Instant

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 28, 2026)
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18407409
We define a universality class of operators, $C_{crit}$, characterized by maximal spectral rigidity and holographic saturation. We prove a Structural Exclusion Theorem: operators in this class cannot admit eigenvalues violating the Critical Line symmetry without breaking the universality of their spacing distribution (GUE). We rigororusly derive this from the "Clustering Anomaly" and the "Entropy Gap", identifying the Riemann Hypothesis as the condition of thermodynamic stability for the informational vacuum.

In the Theory of Structural Solvability, we classify the Riemann Hypothesis as a Class B (Structurally Universal) problem. It cannot be resolved by geometric deduction, but implies a Thermodynamic Selection Principle.

Part I: The Critical Instant

We posit that the Riemann Zeros are not artifacts of a random operator, but spectral signatures of the **unique, inevitable operator** that governs a system at the limit of Maximum Causal Connectivity and Holographic Saturation.

The Critical Instant ($\tau_c$) is the state where:

  1. Metric Compactness: Graph diameter is minimized (Small World).
  2. Maximal Entropy: The system is in thermal equilibrium.
  3. Holographic Saturation: Degrees of freedom scale with the area law.

Part II: Class $C_{crit}$ and Spectral Rigidity

We define the class $\mathcal{C}_{crit}$ of operators satisfying:

  • Logarithmic Weyl Law: Asymptotic growth matches the prime distributon.
  • Spectral Rigidity: The number variance $\Sigma^2(L)$ converges to the GUE limit ($\sim \ln L$).
  • Hard Chaos: The classical dynamics are fully mixing (K-System).

Part III: The Structural Exclusion Theorem

Theorem: No operator $H \in \mathcal{C}_{crit}$ admits eigenvalues strictly off the axis of symmetry.

Proof Strategy:

  1. Clustering Anomaly: Any off-axis zero $\rho = \sigma + i\gamma$ ($\sigma \neq 1/2$) generates a symmetric quadruplet due to the functional equation. This imposes a fixed length scale $\delta_{\sigma} = |2\sigma - 1|$.
  2. Rigidity Breaking: This fixed scale violates the scale-invariant description of the GUE number variance. The local statistics become Poissonian (uncorrelated) at scale $\delta_{\sigma}$.
  3. Entropy Gap: A spectrum with clustering (Poisson) has strictly lower entropy than a rigid spectrum (GUE). Since $C_{crit}$ is defined by Maximum Entropy, such a state is excluded.

Part IV: The Physical Interpretation

If a Riemann Zero existed off the critical line, it would represent a "particle pair" statistically distinguishable from the background vacuum. Under the Entropic Force ($F = T \nabla S$), this fluctuation is unstable. The system evolves to dissolve the cluster, pushing the eigenvalue back to the line of symmetry.

Conclusion

The Riemann Hypothesis is the statement that the vacuum is thermodynamically stable. A violation of RH would imply that the universe can lower its free energy by spontaneously forming order (clustering), which contradicts the Second Law. The Critical Line is the only attractor in the space of spectral operators.
