Analytic Failure Models of String Theory and Loop Quantum Gravity

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We perform a forensic analysis of the two leading candidates for Quantum Gravity: String Theory and Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG). Using the Compatibility Criterion of the Theory of Regime Incompatibility (TRI), we identify the specific axiomatic violations that have prevented empirical success in both programs. String Theory fails by assuming a background metric compatible with quantum excitations (Background Independence Violation). LQG fails by attempting to quantize geometry directly without an emergent mechanism for the continuum limit.

For 40 years, high-energy physics has been dominated by research programs that have yielded mathematical richness but zero physical predictions. TRI explains why.

String Theory Analysis

String Theory posits 1-dimensional objects moving in a target spacetime. While it unifies forces, it suffers from:

1. The Landscape Problem: $10^{500}$ vacua makes the theory non-predictive.

2. Background Dependence: Strings vibrate in spacetime. They do not generate it dynamically in a way that preserves Diff-Invariance at the fundamental level.

TRI Verdict: String Theory attempts to extend the quantum regime ($\mathcal{R}_{QM}$) infinitely to cover gravity, effectively ignoring the phase transition required to generate smooth geometry.

Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) Analysis

LQG attempts to quantize space itself using spin networks.

1. The Area Spectrum: LQG predicts discrete area eigenvalues. While consistent with Bekenstein, it struggles to recover smooth GR in the low-energy limit.

2. Lorentz Invariance: Discretizing space often breaks Lorentz symmetry.

TRI Verdict: LQG attempts to force the geometric regime ($\mathcal{R}_{GR}$) into a Hilbert space structure, violating the incompatibility of their invariants.

Conclusion

Both major programs failed because they tried to unify incompatible regimes rather than describing the transition between them. Gravity is not a string, nor a loop; it is the thermodynamic wake of quantum information processing.
