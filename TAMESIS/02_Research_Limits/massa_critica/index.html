We present a calibrated model for the breakdown of quantum unitarity at macroscopic scales. The critical mass $M_c \approx 5.29 \times 10^{-16}$ kg emerges from 8-dimensional phase space geometry, with a coherence time $\tau_c \approx 2.18$ seconds derived from gravitational self-energy. For masses $M > M_c$, superpositions decay with $\tau(M) = \tau_c (M_c/M)^2$, providing quantitative targets for MAQRO-class experiments and levitated optomechanics.

1. Theoretical Foundation

The critical mass derives from projecting the cosmological acceleration hierarchy onto 8-dimensional relativistic phase space:

$$ M_c = m_P \cdot \left(\frac{a_0}{a_P}\right)^{1/8} $$

where $m_P \approx 2.17 \times 10^{-8}$ kg is the Planck mass, $a_0 \approx 6.8 \times 10^{-10}$ m/s$^2$ is the cosmological acceleration, and $a_P \approx 5.56 \times 10^{51}$ m/s$^2$ is the Planck acceleration.

Why 1/8?

The exponent emerges from phase space dimensionality:

$$ \dim(\mathcal{M}_8) = \dim(x^\mu) + \dim(p_\mu) = 8 $$

Projection from 8D volume to 1D mass yields the eighth root.

2. Calibrated Time Scale

The coherence time is derived from gravitational self-energy (Penrose-like):

$$ \tau_c = \frac{\hbar \cdot R_c}{G \cdot M_c^2} \approx 2.18 \text{ s} $$

For a silica nanosphere at $M_c$ with radius $R_c \approx 386$ nm. Above threshold:

$$ \tau(M) = \tau_c \cdot \left(\frac{M_c}{M}\right)^2 \quad \text{for } M > M_c $$

Falsifiability Criteria

The theory is falsified if:

  1. Stable interference ($V > 50\%$) observed for $M \geq 10^{-14}$ kg at $t > 1$ s
  2. Coherence observed for $M \gg M_c$ at $t \gg \tau(M)$
  3. Mass scaling differs from $\alpha = 2$
  4. Coherence time increases with isolation for $M > 5 \times 10^{-15}$ kg

6. Conclusion

We present a fully calibrated model providing: (1) first-principles derivation of $\tau_c \approx 2.18$ s, (2) quantitative predictions for MAQRO and levitated nanoparticles, (3) discrimination from CSL/GRW/Diósi-Penrose, and (4) clear falsifiable criteria.

The central prediction—a sharp transition at $M_c \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-16}$ kg—is within reach of current technology.
