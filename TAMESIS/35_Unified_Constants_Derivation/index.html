We report the failure of the "Tamesis Action" to provide a unified derivation of the cosmological constant $\Lambda$, the MOND acceleration $a_0$, and the critical collapse mass $M_c$ from a single holographic radius $R_H$. While $\Lambda$ is consistent with the de Sitter horizon by definition, and $a_0$ shows an order-of-magnitude coincidence, the derived value for $M_c$ diverges from observation by 24 orders of magnitude ($10^9$ kg vs $10^{-14}$ kg). This catastrophic failure refutes the central hypothesis of the Tamesis Action as a Theory of Everything, demonstrating that quantum wavefunction collapse is a local phenomenon independent of cosmological scales.
VERDICT: REFUTED

The allure of a Theory of Everything (ToE) often lies in its ability to connect the very large (Cosmology) with the very small (Quantum Mechanics). The Tamesis Action proposed that a single length scale, the Holographic Radius $R_H = c/H_0$, fundamentally determines all other physical constants [1].

In this Letter, we subject this hypothesis to a definitive falsification test. We require that the three key scales observed in nature ($\Lambda$, $a_0$, $M_c$) be derivable from $R_H$ without adjustable parameters.

The Test

Postulate: $R_H$ determines the Universe.

$$\Lambda = \frac{3}{R_H^2}, \quad a_0 = \frac{c^2}{R_H}, \quad M_c = \frac{\hbar R_H}{c^2 l_P}$$

Success Criterion: All derived values must match observations within experimental error.

Results

Analysis of the Failure

The failure of $M_c$ is instructive. The Tamesis prediction assumed that the mass causing collapse is related to the cosmic horizon. However, the observed $M_c \sim 10^{-14}$ kg (nanoparticle scale) depends on local parameters: coherence length and self-gravity [2]. The discrepancy of 24 orders of magnitude is not a calculation error; it is a category error. Quantum collapse is not a cosmological effect.

Conclusion

The Tamesis Action is falsified. $\Lambda$ and $a_0$ may be related through large-scale geometry, but $M_c$ is an independent local scale. Unification of these three requires a theory that accommodates independent regimes [3], not a single holographic formula.
