An Atlas of Universality Classes in Regime Transitions

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Abstract. We present a complete classification (Atlas) of universality classes for regime transitions. By analyzing state space discreteness, perturbation type, and observable algebra, we identify five distinct classes: $U_{1/2}$ (Computational), $U_2$ (Quantum), $U_0$ (Threshold), $U_{exp}$ (Thermodynamic), and $U_{Ising}$ (Lattice). We provide a decision tree for classifying any physical transition into one of these classes. This Atlas serves as the empirical basis for the Theory of Regime Incompatibility.

Universality is the observation that macroscopic behavior naturally groups into discrete classes independent of microscopic details. Here we catalog these classes.

The Origin of Classes

The exponent $\alpha$ is not arbitrary. It encodes the mechanism of the transition:

1. $U_{1/2}$: Arises from counting measures in discrete spaces. The square root comes from random walk statistics on the symmetric group $S_n$.

2. $U_2$: Arises from the Jacobian of eigenvalues in random matrix theory (GUE). The quadratic repulsion is purely geometric.

3. $U_0$: Arises from sharp threshold phenomena where probability functions become step functions in the thermodynamic limit.

This Atlas confirms that universality is about mechanisms, not systems. The same exponent appears in seemingly unrelated fields (e.g., $U_2$ in nuclear physics and number theory) because the underlying invariant mechanism (GUE statistics) is identical.
