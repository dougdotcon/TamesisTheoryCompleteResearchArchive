2.1 Fine Structure Constant (α ≈ 1/137)

Derivation 1: α from Graph Connectivity

The electromagnetic coupling emerges from the topology of charge propagation on the Kernel graph. For a graph with spectral dimension $d_s$ and mean connectivity $k$:

$$ \alpha = \frac{2\pi}{d_s \cdot k \cdot \ln(k)} $$

Result: For $d_s = 4$ (4D spacetime) and $k = 53.97$, we obtain $\alpha^{-1} = 137.04$, matching QED measurements to < 0.01%.

The physical interpretation is that electromagnetic interactions occur via "paths" on the graph, and the coupling strength is determined by how efficiently charge can propagate. The factor $\ln(k)$ arises from the entropic cost of maintaining phase coherence across $k$ neighbors.

2.3 Fermion Mass Hierarchy

$$ m_f = v_{EW} \cdot \varepsilon^{Q_f} $$
