The Threshold Class U(0): The Onset of Classicality

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We define the Universality Class $U_0$ as the critical boundary where quantum coherence vanishes ($V \to 0$). This corresponds to the critical mass $M_c$ derived in earlier works. We model this transition as a Percolation Transition in the Hilbert Space graph. Below $M_c$, quantum states form a giant connected component (Superposition). Above $M_c$, the graph fragments into disconnected islands (Classical States).

Classicality is not a property of large systems per se, but of disconnected systems. The $U_0$ class describes the physics of this disconnection.

Percolation Model

Let $p$ be the probability of a coherent phase link between basis states. We find $p$ scales as $p \sim e^{-S/S_{BH}}$, where $S_{BH}$ is the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.

$$ p_c = \frac{1}{d} $$

When the system size $d$ (dimension) exceeds the connectivity threshold, the "Quantum Web" breaks. This explains why the classical world appears unique: it is the only remaining connected island in a sea of disconnected possibilities.
