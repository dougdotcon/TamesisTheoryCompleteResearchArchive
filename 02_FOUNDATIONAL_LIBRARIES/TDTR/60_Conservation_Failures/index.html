Conservation Failures at Regime Interfaces

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Noether's Theorem links conservation laws to symmetries. When a transition breaks a symmetry, the associated quantity is no longer conserved. We analyze the non-conservation of energy in the Cosmological Transition (breaking of time-translation symmetry) and the non-conservation of unitarity in the Measurement Transition. We show these "failures" are necessary features of regime change.

Energy is not conserved in an expanding universe. This is a feature, not a bug.

The Noether Gap

$$ \partial_\mu J^\mu \neq 0 $$

During a transition $E_{ij}$, the current $J^\mu$ leaks into the "bulk" embedding space. What appears as loss in $R_i$ is gain in $R_j$, or storage in the transition layer itself.
