We detail the computational algorithm used to reconstruct the geometry of spacetime from its spectral data. By inverting the Selberg Trace Formula, we demonstrate how the distribution of prime geodesics (topological cycles) uniquely determines the manifold's shape. The Algorithm

Our computational approach involves three steps:

1. Eigenvalue Generation: We use the Hejhal-Maass algorithm to compute the zeroes of the Selberg Zeta function $Z(s)$.

2. Trace Regularization: We apply spectral smoothing to remove high-frequency noise and isolate the "low-lying" topology (the Standard Model sector).

3. Constant Extraction: We match the spectral gaps $\Delta \lambda_n$ to the physical coupling constants ($\alpha, G_F, \alpha_s$).
