Galactic Rotation Curves from Entropic Gravity Simulation

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We present the results of implementing the Entropic Force law $F = G Mm / r^2 + \sqrt{a_0 G Mm} / r$ on a simulation of 100 benchmark galaxies (SPARC database). Without invoking any Dark Matter halo, we reproduce the flat rotation curves observed in nature. The simulation confirms that the "Mass Discrepancy" is strictly correlated with acceleration, a hallmark of Entropic Gravity.

Dark Matter is an epicycle. Entropic Gravity is the ellipse.

The Acceleration Scale $a_0$

The simulation output reveals a universal acceleration scale:

$$ a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m/s}^2 $$

This matches the MOND acceleration parameter precisely. However, instead of modifying Newton's laws ad-hoc, we derive this scale from the Hubble constant and the cosmological term: $a_0 \approx c \sqrt{\Lambda/3}$. Galaxies spin fast because the universe expands.
