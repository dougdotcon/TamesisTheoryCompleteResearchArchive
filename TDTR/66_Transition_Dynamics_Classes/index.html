Classification of Transition Dynamics

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We classify the dynamic universality classes of regime transitions. Using the framework of Critical Phenomena, we identify three distinct modes of transition: (1) First-Order (Nucleation), (2) Second-Order (Continuous Symmetry Breaking), and (3) Topological (BKT-like). We argue that the Big Bang was a First-Order transition from a pre-geometric phase, while the electroweak symmetry breaking was Second-Order.

Not all changes are created equal. Some are explosions; some are whispers.

Implications for Inflation

The Inflationary Epoch is best modeled as a Type I transition. The "Latent Heat" released is the matter and radiation of the current universe. We are the exhaust of a phase change.
