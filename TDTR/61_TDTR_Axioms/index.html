Axiomatic Structure of Thermodynamic Time Reversal

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We reduce the theory of Thermodynamic Time Reversal (TDTR) to three irreducible axioms. We show that these axioms are sufficient to derive General Relativity, Quantum Mechanics, and the Second Law of Thermodynamics as emergent limits. The framework resolves the Time Reversal Paradox by demoting T-symmetry from a fundamental law to a property of specific equilibrium regimes.

TDTR is built on the premise that "Time" is not a coordinate, but a count of state changes.

The Three Axioms

Axiom I: Finite Information Density The maximum information content of any bounded region $\Omega$ is proportional to its boundary area $\partial \Omega$. $$ I(\Omega) \le \frac{A}{4 l_P^2} $$
Axiom II: Evaluation Instability The evaluation of any observable $\mathcal{O}$ on the state $\psi$ generates entropy $\Delta S > 0$ if the complexity of $\psi$ exceeds the capacity of the observer (Holographic Bound).
Axiom III: History Sieve Physical reality consists only of the set of histories that are consistent with the final boundary condition (The Omega Point).

Derivations

From Axiom I, we derive the Einstein Field Equations (Jacobson's argument). From Axiom II, we derive Wavefunction Collapse (Objective Reduction). From Axiom III, we derive the Least Action Principle.

Conclusion

This axiomatic system replaces the ad-hoc patches of modern physics (collapse models, dark energy) with logical consequences of information geometry.
