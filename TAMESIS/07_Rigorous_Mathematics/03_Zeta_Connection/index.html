Summary This module loads known non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function and compares them with operator eigenvalues. The comparison quantifies how far our naive operator is from the hypothetical "Riemann operator."

1. The Riemann Zeros

The Riemann Hypothesis states that all non-trivial zeros of $\zeta(s)$ lie on the critical line:

$$\rho_n = \frac{1}{2} + i\gamma_n$$

where $\gamma_n$ are real numbers. The first few values are:

Known Zeros (imaginary parts):
$\gamma_1 = 14.134725...$
$\gamma_2 = 21.022040...$
$\gamma_3 = 25.010858...$
$\gamma_4 = 30.424876...$
$\gamma_5 = 32.935062...$

3. Operator Comparison

Comparing Berry-Keating eigenvalues with Riemann zeros:

  • Correlation: Very weak (eigenvalues don't match zeros)
  • Scale: Different order of magnitude
  • Conclusion: Naive discretization fails to reproduce RH spectrum

This is expected. The correct regularization remains an open problem.
