Experimental Probes of the U(1/2) Universality Class

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We propose a suite of experiments designed to detect the signature of the "Intermediate Regime" $U_{1/2}$, a phase of matter residing between Quantum Coherence ($U_0$) and Classical Ergodicity ($U_{exp}$). Standard physics recognizes two main dynamic classes: Integrable (Quantum, Periodic) and Chaotic (Classical, Thermal). The Tamesis system predicts a third, stable class: $U_{1/2}$, governed by the zeros of the Riemann Zeta function.

Signature 1: The Golden Decay

In a standard chaotic system, the Out-of-Time-Order Correlator (OTOC) decays exponentially: $C(t) \sim e^{-\lambda t}$. In the $U_{1/2}$ regime, we predict a recurrence modulation:

$$ C(t) \sim e^{-\lambda t} + \phi \cos(\omega_{zeros} t) $$

where $\phi$ is the Golden Ratio and $\omega_{zeros}$ are the frequencies of the Zeta zeros. This would appear as "quantum scars" in the thermalization profile of a many-body system.

Test 1: Cold Atom Scrambling

Experiment: Trap 1000 Rb-87 atoms in a 1D optical lattice. Quench the system to a critical energy density.
Prediction: The relaxation time will stall at specific intervals corresponding to the prime number distribution, rather than following a smooth thermalization curve.

Test 2: Cavity QCD

Using a high-finesse cavity coupled to a Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC), we can simulate the transition from $U_0$ to $U_{1/2}$. Tamesis predicts a phase transition where the photon number statistics shift from Poissonian to Prime-Number distributions (via the GUE ensemble).

Conclusion

The existence of the $U_{1/2}$ class is falsifiable. If observed, it proves that "Chaos" is not random, but number-theoretically structured.
