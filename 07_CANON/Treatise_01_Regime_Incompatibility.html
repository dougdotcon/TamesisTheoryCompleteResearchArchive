Theory of Regime Incompatibility: The Impossibility of Unification

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 28, 2026)
Abstract> We formally demonstrate that there exists no single axiomatic framework capable of describing all physical regimes simultaneously. We identify three fundamental "No-Go Theorems" that prohibit the unification of discrete and continuous state spaces, as well as the unitary evolution of Quantum Mechanics and the diff-invariance of General Relativity. This establishes the Theory of Regime Incompatibility (TRI), which posits that the universe is a patchwork of effective theories separated by topological phase transitions.

The pursuit of a Theory of Everything (ToE) relies on the assumption that a single master equation can govern physics at all scales. We present evidence that this assumption violates the structural limitations of mathematical logic.

I. The Three No-Go Theorems

Our analysis reveals insurmountable barriers to unification:

1. Discrete-Continuous No-Go

Regimes with discrete state spaces (like quantum spin networks) cannot be smoothly mapped to continuous state spaces (like smooth manifolds) without information loss or generation. Unification requires a non-unitary transformation.

2. Quantum-Gravity No-Go

Quantum Field Theory (QFT) relies on unitary evolution on a fixed background, while General Relativity (GR) requires background independence and diffeomorphism invariance. These invariants are mathematically incompatible. You cannot have both in the same "room".

3. Universality Class Exclusion

Every physical transition belongs to exactly one Universality Class (e.g., Ising, Percolation). There is no "Master Class" that contains the critical exponents of all others. Therefore, a theory optimized for one regime must fail in another.

II. Implication for ToE Candidates

TRI predicts the inevitable failure of traditional unification attempts:

  • String Theory: Fails the Quantum-Gravity No-Go by assuming a fixed background for strings.
  • Loop Quantum Gravity: Fails the Discrete-Continuous No-Go by struggling to recover the continuum limit.
  • Tamesis Action (Early): Failed the Universality Class Exclusion.

III. Conclusion

The key insight of TRI is that the unity of physics is not found in the laws, but in the limits of what can coexist. The universe is not a monolith; it is a modular system where incompatible regimes are separated by boundary conditions.

"There is no single equation, only a graph of theories."
