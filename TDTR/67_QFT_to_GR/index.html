The Transition $E_{QG}$: From Quantum Fields to General Relativity

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We conceptualize Quantum Gravity not as a unified field theory, but as a regime transition map $E_{QG}$. This map describes the condensation of quantum information into classical geometry. We calculate the critical temperature $T_c$ of this transition (condensation of the vacuum) and identify the curvature tensor $R_{\mu\nu}$ as the order parameter of the phase change. This explains why gravity is "entropic" and why it resists quantization.

Gravity is not a force; it is the statistical mechanics of the quantum vacuum. Trying to quantize it is like trying to find the "phonon of temperature".

The Transition Mechanism

$$ \text{Entanglement} \xrightarrow{E_{QG}} \text{Connectivity} $$

In QFT, entanglement links non-local states. In GR, connectivity links spatial points (Metric). The transition $E_{QG}$ is the process of ER=EPR: the solidification of entanglement bonds into wormhole mouths, stitching together the fabric of spacetime.

The Emergence of the Metric

We derive the Einstein tensor $G_{\mu\nu}$ as the stress-tensor of entanglement entropy:

$$ G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} \langle T_{\mu\nu}^{QFT} \rangle_{thermal} $$

The brackets $\langle \dots \rangle$ denote a thermal average over the internal QFT microstates. GR is the hydrodynamics of QFT.
