Selection Rules for Allowed Transitions

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Not all conceivable transitions between physical regimes are physically realizable. We derive the "Selection Rules" of the Transition Algebra, analogous to selection rules in spectroscopy. We show that transitions $R_i \to R_j$ are forbidden if the information loss $\Delta I$ violates the Holevo bound or if the topological charge of the invariants cannot vary continuously.

Can a Classical universe suddenly become Quantum? TDTR says no.

Rule 1: The Entropy Monotone

$\Delta S_{transition} > 0$

A transition is allowed only if it increases the total entropy of the multiverse. This forbids "re-quantization" of a macroscopic classical system (Anti-Decoherence) without massive external work.

Rule 2: Topology Conservation

Transitions cannot change global topological invariants (e.g., Euler characteristic) unless fueled by a singularity. This protects the stability of the vacuum against arbitrary decay.
