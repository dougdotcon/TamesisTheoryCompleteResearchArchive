RIEMANN HYPOTHESIS

What Self-Adjointness Means

An operator \(D\) is self-adjoint if:

\[\langle Df, g \rangle = \langle f, Dg \rangle \quad \text{for all } f, g \text{ in the domain}\]

and the domain of \(D\) equals the domain of \(D^*\).

For self-adjoint operators, all eigenvalues are real. This is why \(D = D^*\) implies RH.

The Connes Obstruction

The space \(L^2(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Q}^*)\) has a "trivial subspace" where \(D\) is NOT self-adjoint.

The regularization must:

  1. Remove this subspace
  2. Preserve \(\text{Tr}_{\text{reg}} =\) Weil formula
  3. Keep \(D\) self-adjoint in the residual space

Connes showed (1) and (2). Step (3) is the open problem.
