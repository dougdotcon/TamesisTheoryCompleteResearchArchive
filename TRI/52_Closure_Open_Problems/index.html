Theory of Regime Incompatibility: Closure and Open Problems

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We summarize the foundational success of the Theory of Regime Incompatibility (TRI) in proving the impossibility of a unitary unification of Quantum Mechanics and General Relativity. We have defined regimes, established the Incompatibility Criterion, and cataloged the failure modes of previous theories. However, TRI describes a static graph of constraints. It does not yet describe the dynamics of the edges. We outline the open problems that necessitate the subsequent specialized theories: TDTR (for dynamics) and TAMESIS (for the specific mathematical structure of our universe).

TRI has served its purpose: it has cleared the ground of impossible expectations. We theoretically proved that the Universe is not a single monolith but a patchwork of regimes.

Summary of TRI Achievements

1. Regime Definition: Physics occurs in $(\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{I})$ triples.

2. Incompatibility Proof: $QM \neq GR$ due to invariant mismatch.

3. Local Universality: Constants are regime-specific invariants.

Open Problems

Problem 1: The Dynamics of Transitions
TRI says transitions happen, but not how. What is the equation of motion for a transition $E_{ij}$? How does information flow across the boundary?
$\to$ This requires Thermodynamic Time Reversal (TDTR).
Problem 2: The Specific Topology of Our Universe
TRI describes general regimes. Why does our universe use the specific constants and invariants it does? Is there a unifying geometric principle underlying the regime currents?
$\to$ This requires The Tamesis System.
Problem 3: The Critical Mass ($M_c$)
TRI predicts a boundary between Quantum and Classical. What is the precise numerical value of this boundary, and how do we test it?
$\to$ Addressed in Tamesis Paper 35 & 42.

The Roadmap Forward

The completion of TRI moves our focus to the "Engine Room" of the theory. We must now derive the specific transition amplitudes (TDTR) and the holographic geometry (TAMESIS) that implement the constraints discovered here.

Conclusion

The Theory of Regime Incompatibility is complete. It stands as the "No-Go" guardian of the TARDIS framework, ensuring we do not waste time on mathematically impossible unifications. The path is now open for the constructive phase.
