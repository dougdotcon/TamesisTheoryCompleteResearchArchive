The Cusp Scattering Phase of the Modular Surface
and the Logarithmic Term in Riemann-von Mangoldt

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Abstract

We establish a precise decomposition of the scattering phase $\Theta(T)$ for the modular surface $M = SL(2,\mathbb{Z}) \backslash \mathbb{H}$. The main result shows that the $T \log T$ term comes entirely from the zeta function ratio (arithmetic), while the Gamma contribution is bounded, converging to $-1/4$. This connects spectral theory to the Riemann-von Mangoldt formula.

Keywords: Selberg trace formula, scattering matrix, Riemann zeta, modular surface

1. Introduction

For the modular surface $M = SL(2,\mathbb{Z}) \backslash \mathbb{H}$, the scattering matrix is explicit:

$$\phi(s) = \sqrt{\pi} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(s - 1/2) \cdot \zeta(2s - 1)}{\Gamma(s) \cdot \zeta(2s)}$$

The scattering phase $\Theta(T) = \frac{1}{\pi} \arg \phi(1/2 + iT)$ contributes to spectral counting, revealing the interplay between geometry and arithmetic.

2. Main Theorem

Theorem (Main Result)

For $M = SL(2,\mathbb{Z}) \backslash \mathbb{H}$:

$$\Theta(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi} \log\frac{T}{2\pi} - \frac{T}{2\pi} + E(T)$$

where $E(T) = E_\Gamma + E_\zeta$ with:

  • $E_\Gamma = -\frac{1}{4} + O(1/T)$ (analytic, bounded)
  • $E_\zeta = O(\log T)$ (arithmetic, from zeros)

4. Corollaries

Corollary 1: The $T \log T$ term comes ENTIRELY from the zeta ratio.

Corollary 2: The Gamma contribution is bounded: $\Theta_\Gamma \to -1/4$.

Corollary 3: The "excess" beyond Weyl is arithmetic (encodes prime distribution).

6. Conclusion

We have established that the $T \log T$ term in the modular scattering phase is purely arithmetic. The Gamma contribution is purely analytic and bounded. This provides an explicit mechanism connecting spectral theory to the distribution of Riemann zeros.
