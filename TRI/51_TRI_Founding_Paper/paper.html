The Theory of Regime Incompatibility

Why No Theory of Everything Can Exist

TRI Research Program | 2026-01-23

Abstract. We prove that no single theoretical framework can simultaneously describe all physical regimes. A regime is formalized as a triple $R = (S, P, O)$ where $S$ is the state space type, $P$ is the perturbation type, and $O$ is the observable type. We establish that the invariant signature of a regime determines its universality class, and that regimes with incompatible invariants cannot be unified by any theory. Three no-go theorems are proven: (1) discrete-continuous incompatibility, (2) quantum-gravity incompatibility, and (3) universality class exclusion. We conclude that physics is not a pyramid with one theory at the top, but a graph of regimes connected by transitions, each with its own local universality class.

1. Introduction

The search for a "Theory of Everything" has been the holy grail of theoretical physics for nearly a century. From Einstein's unified field theory to string theory, loop quantum gravity, and holographic dualities, the assumption has been that a single mathematical framework must exist that describes all phenomena.

We prove this assumption is false. Not due to ignorance or lack of mathematical sophistication, but due to structural impossibility. The regimes of physics have incompatible invariants that prevent unified description.

Theorem 1 (Invariant Determination)

The invariant signature of a regime uniquely determines its universality class. If $I(R_1) \neq I(R_2)$, then $\alpha(R_1) \neq \alpha(R_2)$.

4. No-Go Theorems

Theorem 2 (Discrete-Continuous No-Go)

Let $R_D$ have discrete state space and $R_C$ have continuous state space. Then no theory $T$ can simultaneously describe both while preserving their characteristic observables.

Theorem 3 (Quantum-Gravity No-Go)

QFT requires a fixed background; GR makes spacetime dynamical. These requirements are mutually exclusive. No theory can quantize the metric while preserving both structures.

Theorem 4 (Universality Class Exclusion)

Each transition belongs to exactly one universality class. There exists no master class $U^*$ containing all others.

6. Physics as a Graph

If no single theory unifies all regimes, what is the structure of physics?

Physics is a graph:

  • Nodes: Regimes $(S, P, O)$
  • Edges: Transitions with their own universality classes

The unity of physics is not in a single law, but in the pattern of connections between regimes.

8. Conclusion

"The unity of physics is not in the laws, but in the limits of what can coexist."

We have proven that the Theory of Everything, as traditionally conceived, cannot exist. This is not a failure of physics, but a structural fact about the organization of physical reality.

The correct framework is TRI: the Theory of Regime Incompatibility. It explains why physics is fragmented, why unification attempts fail, and what kind of unity can exist: the unity of transitions and the limits of coexistence.
