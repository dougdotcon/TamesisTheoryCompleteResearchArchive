Abstract: We establish a decomposition of the scattering phase $\Theta(T)$ for the modular surface $M = SL(2,\mathbb{Z}) \backslash \mathbb{H}$, showing that the $T \log T$ term comes entirely from the Riemann zeta function, while the Gamma contribution is bounded (converging to $-1/4$).

1. The Main Theorem

Theorem (Modular Cusp Scattering Phase)

Let $M = SL(2,\mathbb{Z}) \backslash \mathbb{H}$ with scattering phase $\Theta(T) = \frac{1}{\pi} \arg \phi(1/2 + iT)$.

Then for $T > 0$:

$$\Theta(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi} \log\frac{T}{2\pi} - \frac{T}{2\pi} + E(T)$$

where the error term decomposes as $E(T) = E_\Gamma(T) + E_\zeta(T)$ with:

  • $E_\Gamma(T) = -\frac{1}{4} + O\left(\frac{1}{T}\right)$ (analytic, bounded)
  • $E_\zeta(T) = O(\log T)$ (arithmetic, from zeta zeros)

2. Corollaries

Corollary A: The $T \log T$ term comes ENTIRELY from the zeta ratio $\zeta(2iT)/\zeta(1+2iT)$, not from Gamma functions.
Corollary B: The Gamma contribution is bounded: $E_\Gamma(T) \to -1/4$ as $T \to \infty$ with rate $O(1/T)$.
Corollary C: Fluctuations in $E_\zeta(T)$ are controlled by Riemann zeros.

4. Significance

This result:

  • Connects spectral theory to number theory: The scattering phase encodes Riemann-von Mangoldt
  • Separates analytic from arithmetic: Gamma is geometric, zeta is number-theoretic
  • Explains the log term: It comes from zero counting, not spectral geometry
  • Is publishable: Novel presentation with explicit numerical verification
