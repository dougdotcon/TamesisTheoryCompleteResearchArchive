## 1. Introduction: The Pivot

The Tamesis Program began with the ambition to unify Quantum Mechanics and General Relativity into a single mathematical framework (a "Theory of Everything"). Through rigorous analysis and computational stress-testing, we discovered that **such a unification is mathematically impossible** within a single manifold.

This failure led to a paradigm shift: instead of trying to *force* compatibility, we developed a theory to describe the *incompatibility* itself. This resulted in three distinct, interlocking layers of new physics.

### What is it?

TRI explains why a Theory of Everything fails. It posits that the universe is comprised of distinct **Physical Regimes** (e.g., The Quantum Regime, The Relativistic Regime) that are topologically distinct. You cannot transform one into the other continuously; you must pass through a "Phase Transition."

### Core Discovery: The No-Go Theorems

We proved three fundamental theorems:

1. **The Incompatibility Theorem:** A vacuum state cannot simultaneously optimize for Locality (GR) and Unitarity (QM).
2. **The Horizon Theorem:** Information cannot be conserved across a regime boundary without a change in dimensionality.
3. **The Transition Theorem:** To move from one regime to another requires a non-unitary operation (a break in symmetry).

### Implications

- "String Theory" describes one specific regime. "Loop Quantum Gravity" describes another. Both are correct locally, neither is correct globally.
- The universe is a "patchwork" of effective field theories sewn together by domain walls.
