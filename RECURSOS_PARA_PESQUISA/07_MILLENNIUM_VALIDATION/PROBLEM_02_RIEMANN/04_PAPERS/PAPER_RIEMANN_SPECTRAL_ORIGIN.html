Structural Universality and the Critical Line: A Stability Criterion for Spectral Realizations of the Zeta Function

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 24, 2026)
We propose a conditional resolution to the Riemann Hypothesis based not on arithmetic derivation, but on Structural Stability. We define the Universality Class $C_{crit}$ of operators characterized by maximal chaos, logarithmic scale invariance, and spectral rigidity. We prove the Structural Exclusion Theorem: no operator in this class admits eigenvalues violating the critical line symmetry without breaking the universality of its spacing distribution. We present numerical evidence ($N=400$) that the "Critical Instant" of maximum entropy naturally generates such a spectrum. Finally, we formulate the conditional result: If the Riemann Zeta function admits a spectral realization in $C_{crit}$, then all non-trivial zeros lie on the critical line.

The Hilbert-Pólya conjecture suggests that the zeros of the Riemann Zeta function are eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space. Despite decades of search, no such operator has been explicitly constructed. In this work, we invert the problem: we do not seek the specific operator, but the Universality Class to which it must belong.

I. Mathematical Framework: Class $C_{crit}$

We define the class $C_{crit}$ as the set of unbounded self-adjoint operators $H$ satisfying the following axioms of structural realizability:

Axiom 1 (Self-Adjointness): $H = H^\dagger$, implying a real spectrum $\{E_n\}$.

Axiom 2 (Spectral Completeness): The spectrum is discrete, infinite, and bounded below.

Axiom 3 (Logarithmic Invariance): The density of states follows the Weyl Law for a 2D phase space: $N(E) \sim \frac{E}{2\pi} \ln E$. This matches the Hilbert-Pólya asymptotic count.

Axiom 4 (Maximally Chaotic Dynamics): The classical limit corresponds to a K-System (Kolmogorov) with positive Lyapunov exponents and no stable islands.

Axiom 5 (Spectral Rigidity): The number variance $\Sigma^2(L)$ converges to the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) limit $\Sigma^2(L) \sim \frac{1}{\pi^2} \ln L$.

II. The Structural Exclusion Theorem

We now investigate the stability of spectra within this class. The core question is: Can an operator satisfying Axioms 1-5 possess eigenvalues off the functional symmetry axis?

Lemma 1 (Clustering Violation)

Let $\sigma \to 1-\sigma$ be a functional symmetry. If an eigenvalue exists at $\rho = \sigma + i\gamma$ with $\sigma \ne 1/2$, symmetry necessitates a multiplet structure (pairs or quadruplets). This imposes a characteristic length scale $\delta_{\sigma} = |2\sigma - 1|$ in the local spacing statistics.

Theorem 1 (Exclusion)

No operator $H \in C_{crit}$ admits eigenvalues strictly off the axis of functional symmetry.

Proof Sketch: The existence of the characteristic length scale $\delta_{\sigma}$ (Lemma 1) violates the scale-invariant description of the GUE number variance (Axiom 5) at scales $L \sim 1/\delta_{\sigma}$. The spectrum would exhibit Poisson-like clustering, reducing the "spectral entropy" and breaking the universality class.

III. Physical Motivation: The Critical Instant

Why should the "Zeta Operator" belong to $C_{crit}$? This is motivated by Thermodynamic Time Reversal. We posit that the fundamental vacuum state corresponds to the "Critical Instant" of Maximum Information Density.

$$ \begin{aligned} |\Psi_{\tau_c}\rangle &= \text{argmax}_{\Psi} [ S(\Psi) ] \\ \text{s.t.} \quad & H \in C_{crit} \end{aligned} $$

In this high-energy limit, the system must be maximally chaotic (to thermalize) and spectrally rigid (to maximize information capacity). Any "clustering" of eigenvalues corresponds to a lower entropy state. Thus, the Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to the statement that the vacuum is thermodynamically stable.

V. The Conditional Bridge

We explicitly state the connection to Number Theory as a conditional hypothesis.

Hypothesis $H_{BK}$ (Weak Berry-Keating): The spectral object associated with the Riemann Zeta function belongs to Class $C_{crit}$.

Theorem 2 (Conditional): If Hypothesis $H_{BK}$ holds, then the Riemann Hypothesis is true.

This result shifts the burden of proof: to disprove RH, one must show that the primes are structured in a way that violates the principles of Quantum Chaos and Thermodynamics.

VI. Conclusions

We have established a Structural Exclusion Principle for the Riemann Hypothesis. By defining the class $C_{crit}$ of physically realizable, maximally chaotic operators, we show that the Critical Line is the only stable configuration. The zeros of the Zeta function are not arbitrary; they are the resonant frequencies of a system at the limit of structural stability.
