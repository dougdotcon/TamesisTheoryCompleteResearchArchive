Chaotic Flow and the Distribution of Geodesic Primes

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
The Prime Number Theorem describes the asymptotic density of natural primes. We derive the "Prime Geodesic Theorem" for the geodesic flow on negative curvature surfaces (Hyperbolic Chaos). We identify the Lyapunov exponent of the flow with the pole of the zeta function, establishing a direct link between the chaotic instability of trajectories and the distribution of stable periodic orbits.

Chaos divides. Order unites. The Zeta function counts the stable islands in the sea of chaos.

The Prime Geodesic Theorem

$$ \pi(x) \sim \text{Li}(e^x) $$

Where $\pi(x)$ is the number of primitive closed geodesics of length less than $x$. This formula is almost identical to the distribution of arithmetic primes, suggesting that quantum chaos is "Arithmetic".

Simulation

The script in this folder simulates the geodesic flow on the modular surface. We observe orbits wrapping around the cusps, corresponding to the excursion of particles near the event horizon.
