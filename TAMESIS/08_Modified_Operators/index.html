Stage 8: Modified Operators

Testing H = xp + V(x) and Understanding Why It Fails
Tamesis Theory Research Archive
January 2026
Summary We tested 13 different potentials V(x) added to the Berry-Keating operator. None produced eigenvalues matching Riemann zeros. Scaling analysis reveals a fundamental mismatch between discretized operator spectra and the Riemann zero distribution.

1. Potentials Tested

Potential Formula Best E₁ Target γ₁
Harmonic $V(x) = \omega^2 x^2$ 0.27 14.13
Coulomb $V(x) = -Z/x$ 0.63 14.13
Logarithmic $V(x) = \lambda \log x$ 0.07 14.13
Inverse Square $V(x) = \alpha/x^2$ 0.60 14.13
Linear $V(x) = mx$ 0.24 14.13

Result: All eigenvalues are O(1), not O(10). Error ≈ 98%.

2. Scaling Analysis

The fundamental mismatch:

Discretized spectrum: $E_n \sim \frac{n}{b-a}$ (linear growth)

Riemann zeros: $\gamma_n \sim \frac{n \log n}{2\pi}$ (logarithmic correction)
Scale factor needed: ~30×
First discretized eigenvalue: E₁ ≈ 0.2
First Riemann zero: γ₁ = 14.13

3. No-Go Conclusion

NO FINITE-DIFFERENCE DISCRETIZATION OF xp + V(x)
CAN REPRODUCE RIEMANN ZEROS

This is a structural impossibility, not a parameter tuning problem.

Implications for the Hilbert-Pólya program:

  • The operator (if it exists) requires infinite domain
  • Or non-standard quantization (adèlic, noncommutative)
  • Or perhaps does not exist at all
