1. The Vacuum Catastrophe

Quantum field theory predicts a vacuum energy density:

$$\rho_{\text{QFT}} \sim \frac{E_{\text{Planck}}^4}{\hbar^3 c^5} \sim 10^{113} \text{ J/m}^3$$

However, cosmological observations constrain:

$$\rho_{\Lambda,\text{obs}} = \frac{\Lambda c^4}{8\pi G} \sim 10^{-9} \text{ J/m}^3$$

The discrepancy is $10^{122}$ — the worst prediction in physics history.

The Tamesis Solution: The vacuum energy is not determined by local QFT, but by the holographic bound on the cosmic horizon.

2. The Holographic Boundary Term

The Tamesis Action includes a boundary term:

$$S_\partial = \frac{A}{4 l_P^2}$$

For the cosmic Hubble horizon with radius $R_H = c/H_0$:

Hubble Horizon Area
$$A_H = 4\pi R_H^2 = 4\pi \frac{c^2}{H_0^2}$$

The maximum entropy the universe can contain is:

$$S_{\text{max}} = \frac{A_H}{4 l_P^2} = \frac{\pi c^2}{H_0^2 l_P^2} = \frac{\pi c^5}{G \hbar H_0^2}$$

3. Derivation of $\Lambda$

Step 1: Entropy Density

The entropy density inside the Hubble volume $V_H = \frac{4}{3}\pi R_H^3$ is:

$$s = \frac{S_{\text{max}}}{V_H} = \frac{3}{4 R_H l_P^2} = \frac{3 H_0}{4 c l_P^2}$$

Step 2: Information Pressure

Following the Verlinde entropic gravity framework, information has an associated pressure:

$$P_{\text{info}} = -T \cdot s = -\frac{\hbar H_0}{2\pi k_B} \cdot \frac{3 H_0}{4 c l_P^2}$$

Where the Gibbons-Hawking temperature of the de Sitter horizon is $T = \hbar H_0 / (2\pi k_B)$.

Step 3: Effective Cosmological Constant

The negative pressure acts as dark energy with equation of state $w = -1$:

$$\rho_\Lambda = -P_{\text{info}} = \frac{3 \hbar H_0^2}{8\pi c l_P^2} = \frac{3 H_0^2 c^2}{8\pi G}$$

Therefore:

Cosmological Constant from Holography
$$\boxed{\Lambda = \frac{3 H_0^2}{c^2}}$$
