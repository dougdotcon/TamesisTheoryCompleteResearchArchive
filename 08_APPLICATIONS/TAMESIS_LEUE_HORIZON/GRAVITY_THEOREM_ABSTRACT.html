We propose that the inverse-square law of gravity is not a fundamental postulate, but a necessary condition for the structural stability of a 3D vacuum manifold. By defining a "Structural Stress Functional" $\mathcal{S}[\alpha]$ associated with the oscillation damping of the vacuum, we demonstrate via the Calculus of Variations that the condition of minimal stress ($\delta \mathcal{S} = 0$) under isotropic symmetry significantly restricts the class of allowed potentials. We prove that the only solution satisfying both stability (boundedness at infinity) and continuity at the source is the Harmonic Potential $\Phi \propto 1/r$, which yields a force $F \propto 1/r^2$. This result suggests that General Relativity may be the effective field theory of a deeper control system that continually minimizes the informational stress of spacetime.

The search for a fundamental derivation of Gravity has occupied physics for centuries. Here we present a short proof demonstrating that the Newtonian potential is strictly required by stability constraints in an isotropic 3D medium.

I. The Stability Theorem

Consider a scalar stiffness field $\alpha(\mathbf{r})$ in $D=3$ dimensions representing the damping capacity of the vacuum. We postulate that the vacuum minimizes its internal "Structural Stress" or Dirichlet energy:

$$ \mathcal{S}[\alpha] = \int_V (\nabla \alpha)^2 dV $$

Applying the Calculus of Variations, the stationary condition $\delta \mathcal{S} = 0$ yields the Euler-Lagrange equation:

$$ \nabla^2 \alpha = 0 $$

This is Laplace's Equation. For an isotropic point source (spherical symmetry), the general solution in 3D is well known:

$$ \alpha(r) = \frac{C_1}{r} + C_2 $$

Requiring the field to vanish at infinity ($C_2=0$) for boundedness, we recover the fundamental potential form $\Phi \sim 1/r$. The corresponding force field is the gradient:

$$ \vec{F} = -\nabla \alpha \propto \frac{1}{r^2} \hat{r} $$

II. Implication

This simple derivation proves that Gravity is the unique stable solution for a damping field in our dimension. Any other exponent (e.g., $1/r^{2+\epsilon}$) would violate the minimization of structural stress, leading to a vacuum state that is either explosive or collapsing.
