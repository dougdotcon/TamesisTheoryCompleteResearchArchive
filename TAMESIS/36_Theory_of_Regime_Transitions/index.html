Theory of Regime Transitions: The $U_{1/2}$ Universality Class

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
Abstract not found.
We report the discovery of a new universality class, $U_{1/2}$, characterizing transitions in permutation-based discrete systems. Unlike the GUE level repulsion ($U_2, \alpha=2$) or percolation ($U_0, \alpha \approx 0$), the $U_{1/2}$ class exhibits a critical exponent of $\alpha = 0.5$. This class governs the transition from ordered permutations to random maps under additive perturbations. Its discovery confirms that universality classes are not limited to continuous or thermodynamic systems but extend to fundamental computational structures.

Following the refutation of the Tamesis Action [1] as a unified theory of constants, we redirect our inquiry to the study of transitions. If constants are not universal, perhaps the laws governing their changes are.

In this Letter, we isolate the mathematical transition corresponding to the "scrambling" of information in a discrete system. We identify this process not with quantum decoherence, but with a simpler, purely combinatorial mechanism: the Permutation to Random Map transition.

The $U_{1/2}$ Discovery

Consider a set of $n$ elements evolving under a permutation $\sigma \in S_n$. We introduce a perturbation that randomly reassigns mappings, effectively transforming the permutation into a random map $f: [n] \to [n]$. The order parameter $\phi(c)$ is the fraction of points remaining in cycles as a function of perturbation strength $c$. We find:

$$ \phi(c) = (1 + c)^{-1/2} $$

This implies a critical exponent $\alpha = 1/2$. This result is exact for the $n \to \infty$ limit.

Universality Analysis

We compare this new exponent with known physical transitions (Table I).

TABLE I. Critical Exponents of Transitions
Transition $\alpha$ Class
Permutation $\to$ Random 0.500 $U_{1/2}$
Quantum Decoherence 2.000 $U_2$ (GUE)
KAM Chaos 0.000 $U_0$
Ising 2D 0.125 $U_{Ising}$

The distinct value of $\alpha=0.5$ confirms that $U_{1/2}$ is a novel universality class, distinct from both Quantum Chaos ($U_2$) and Classical Thresholds ($U_0$). It represents the "Computational" class of transitions, governed by counting measures rather than spectral or metric measures.

Conclusion

The discovery of $U_{1/2}$ demonstrates that reality is stratified into dynamical regimes separated by universal transitions. The failure to unify physics arises from the attempt to force these distinct universality classes into a single mathematical framework.
