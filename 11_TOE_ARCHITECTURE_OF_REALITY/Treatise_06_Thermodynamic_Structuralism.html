The Principles of Thermodynamic Structuralism: The Realizability Constraint on Mathematical Existence

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 28, 2026)
We introduce Thermodynamic Structuralism (TSR), a framework that investigates the constraints imposed by physical laws on mathematical structures. We define the Category of Realizability ($\mathcal{R}$) as the sub-category of mathematical structures that admit a finite, causal, and thermodynamically bounded representation. We argue that "existence" in physics is a subset of "existence" in mathematics, filtered by the Thermodynamic Cost of Information Processing.

Classical physics assumes mathematics is a language to describe the world. TSR inverts this: The World is a computer that executes Mathematical Structures. However, due to finite resources, not all programs can run.

Part I: The Axioms of Realizability

Let $\mathcal{U}$ be the universe of all mathematically consistent structures (ZFC). We define a filter function $\Phi: \mathcal{U} \to \{0, 1\}$ based on the following axioms:

Axiom R1 (Informational Finitude)

A structure $S$ is realizable only if its Kolmogorov Complexity $K(S)$ is finite ($K(S) < \infty$). Non-computable reals are physically excluded.

Axiom R2 (Causal Executability)

The dynamics of $S$ must be representable by a local update rule on a graph with finite propagation speed $c$. Instantaneous action-at-a-distance is forbidden.

Axiom R3 (Spectral Stability)

The Hamiltonian $H_S$ associated with $S$ must possess a spectral gap $\Delta > 0$ that does not vanish exponentially with system size. Unstable chaos is filtered out.

Axiom R4 (Thermodynamic Boundedness)

The action (Energy $\times$ Time) required to instantiate the object must not diverge. $$ \lim_{N \to \infty} \mathcal{C}(\phi) \in O(N^k) $$ Structures with exponential costs are "Censored".

Part II: The Reality Filter

Why does the universe look the way it does? TSR argues that the "Laws of Physics" are survivorship biases of the Realizability Filter.

The Landauer Wall:
If a computation requires more energy than the local free energy budget to process the next bit, the process halts.

Class $\mathcal{NR}$ (Non-Realizable): Structures with exponential cost (e.g., NP-Hard ground states, Singularities). These are censored.

Class $\mathcal{R}$ (Realizable): Structures with polynomial cost (e.g., Stable Matter, P-Time Algorithms). These persist.

Part III: The TSR Test

We apply this framework to unsolved problems via the TSR Test:

  1. Map the problem to a physical operator (Hamiltonian).
  2. Evolve the system under entropic pressure.
  3. Measure the stability (Gap Scaling, Variance).

Result 1 (P vs NP): 3SAT belongs to Class $\mathcal{NR}$ due to Spin Glass instability (Exponential Gap Closure).

Result 2 (Riemann): The Zeta Zeros belong to Class $\mathcal{R}$ due to GUE Spectral Rigidity (Gap Stability).

Conclusion

Thermodynamic Structuralism bridges the gap between Platonism and Empiricism. Mathematical objects "exist" abstractly, but only those maximizing information capacity (Entropy) are physically stable enough to manifest as reality.
