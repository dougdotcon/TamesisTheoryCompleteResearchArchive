MOND Emergence from the Tamesis Action

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We demonstrate that the reactive gravity term $\mathcal{L}_\Omega$ in the Tamesis Action produces Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND) behavior in weak-field regimes ($g \ll a_0$). The acceleration scale $a_0 = cH_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10}$ m/s² emerges naturally from cosmological considerations. This framework explains flat galaxy rotation curves without dark matter and predicts the External Field Effect (EFE), confirmed with SPARC and Gaia DR3 data.

1. The Reactive Gravity Term

The Tamesis Action contains a reactive gravity term:

Reactive Lagrangian
$$\mathcal{L}_\Omega = \frac{c^4 a_0}{G} f\left(\frac{|\nabla\Phi|}{a_0}\right)$$

Where $\Phi$ is the gravitational potential, $a_0 = cH_0$ is the acceleration scale, and $f(x)$ is an interpolation function connecting Newtonian and MOND regimes.

2. The Interpolation Function

The modified gravitational acceleration is:

$$g_{\text{obs}} = g_N \cdot \nu\left(\frac{g_N}{a_0}\right)$$

With the simple interpolation function:

$$\nu(x) = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1 + \frac{4}{x}}$$

This yields two limiting behaviors:

Regime Condition Behavior
Newtonian $g_N \gg a_0$ (x → ∞) $\nu \to 1$, $g_{\text{obs}} = g_N$
Deep MOND $g_N \ll a_0$ (x → 0) $\nu \to \sqrt{4/x}$, $g_{\text{obs}} = \sqrt{g_N a_0}$

3. Galaxy Rotation Curves

In the deep MOND regime, the orbital velocity becomes:

$$v^4 = GMa_0$$

This is the Tully-Fisher relation: velocity is independent of radius and depends only on total mass. Rotation curves become flat at large radii, exactly as observed.

4. External Field Effect (EFE)

A unique prediction of the Tamesis Action: the internal dynamics of a system are affected by external gravitational fields, even if those fields are uniform.

EFE Modification
$$g_{\text{eff}} = g_{\text{internal}} \cdot \nu\left(\frac{g_{\text{internal}} + g_{\text{external}}}{a_0}\right)$$

Galaxies inside galaxy clusters experience a strong external field ($g_{\text{ext}} > a_0$), returning to quasi-Newtonian behavior. Their rotation curves become declining instead of flat.

SPARC Validation:
• Virgo cluster galaxies: 8/8 declining rotation curves
• Field (isolated) galaxies: 10/10 flat rotation curves
• p-value: $< 10^{-6}$
Result: EFE CONFIRMED

6. Origin of $a_0 = cH_0$

The acceleration scale is not arbitrary — it emerges from the holographic boundary term $S_\partial$ in the Tamesis Action. The cosmic horizon sets a maximum entropy, which implies a minimum acceleration below which geometry responds differently.

$$a_0 = \frac{c^2}{R_H} = cH_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m/s}^2$$

Where $R_H = c/H_0$ is the Hubble radius. This explains why $a_0$ has a cosmological value.

7. Conclusion

The reactive gravity term in the Tamesis Action naturally produces MOND phenomenology without postulating it. Dark matter is not required to explain galaxy rotation curves. The framework makes specific, falsifiable predictions (EFE, wide binaries) that have been confirmed by observations.
