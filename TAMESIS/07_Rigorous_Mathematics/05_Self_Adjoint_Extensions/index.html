The Central Question Does there exist a domain $D(H)$ such that the Berry-Keating operator $H = xp$ becomes self-adjoint with discrete spectrum matching Riemann zeros?

This is the real problem. Not RH. Not physics. This.

1. Von Neumann Extension Theory

For a symmetric operator $H$ on domain $D(H)$, the von Neumann theorem states:

$H$ has self-adjoint extensions $\Leftrightarrow$ $n_+ = n_-$

where $n_\pm = \dim \ker(H^* \mp iI)$ (deficiency indices)
Our Result: For $H = xp$ on $L^2([a,b], dx)$:
$(n_+, n_-) = (1, 1)$ → Self-adjoint extensions EXIST

The extensions form a U(1) family parameterized by $\theta \in [0, 2\pi)$.

3. Why Discretization Fails

  • Finite interval: True operator acts on $\mathbb{R}^+$
  • Boundary artifacts: Our BC are artificial
  • No regularization: The "correct" BC remains unknown

To prove RH via this approach requires analytical methods, not numerics.
