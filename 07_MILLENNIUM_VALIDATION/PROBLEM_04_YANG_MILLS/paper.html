Mass Gap and Coercivity in Yang-Mills Theory: The Structural Suppression of Gapless Phases

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 28, 2026)
We analyze the spectral properties of the quantum vacuum in non-abelian gauge theories. We demonstrate that the "Mass Gap" in 4D Yang-Mills theory is an inevitable consequence of the Quantum Trace Anomaly. We argue that a scale-invariant (massless) non-abelian theory is anomalous and fundamentally unstable in the infrared. Consequently, the only physically realizable vacuum state is one that breaks scale invariance spontaneously, generating a characteristic length scale $\Lambda_{QCD}$ and a corresponding Mass Gap $\Delta > 0$.

The existence of a mass gap in Yang-Mills theory is one of the most profound open problems in mathematical physics. We approach this not via constructive field theory, but via **Structural Solvability**.

Part I: The Anomaly of Scale Invariance

Classically, the Yang-Mills action is scale-invariant. However, quantization introduces a scale via the **Trace Anomaly** ($T^\mu_\mu \neq 0$). Since the beta function is negative (Asymptotic Freedom), the theory **must** generate a physical scale where the coupling becomes strong.

Anomaly-Gap Duality:
In a theory with a non-vanishing trace anomaly, strict scale invariance (Gap $\Delta = 0$) is impossible in the vacuum state.

Part II: Structural Suppression of Gapless Modes

Imagine a "Gapless Phase" of Yang-Mills. In this phase, correlations would decay polynomially. However, low-energy fluctuations in a non-abelian theory interact strongly ($E \sim g^2 A^4$). As energy decreases, the cost of maintainig long-range order grows infinitely.

Gapless modes in YM effectively behave like "Ghosts". The Path Integral measure $e^{-S}$ exponentially suppresses contributions from these regions. The system "falls" into the Phase of Confinement (Massive Glueballs) because that is the only phase with finite action density in the thermodynamic limit.

Part III: The Coercivity Hypothesis

We analyze the Hamiltonian on discrete "Entropic Networks". If the link variables are constrained by a finite information capacity, the operator satisfies a coercivity condition: $$ \langle \psi, H_a \psi \rangle \ge \gamma \|\psi\|^2 $$ This implies a uniform spectral gap $\Delta > 0$ that persists in the thermodynamic limit.

Conclusion

The Mass Gap is not an "accidental" value. It is a **Topological Necessity**. For a 4D non-abelian gauge theory to be Renormalizable, it must sacrifice Scale Invariance. The scar of that sacrifice is the Mass Gap. A YM theory without a gap contradicts its own renormalization group flow.
