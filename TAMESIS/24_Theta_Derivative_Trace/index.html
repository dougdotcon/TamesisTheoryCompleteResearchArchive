Stage 24: Theta' as Smooth Trace

Stage 24: \(\Theta'(T)\) as the Smooth Part of the Trace

Result: The derivative of the Riemann-Siegel theta function \(\Theta'(T)\) emerges as the smooth part of the regularized trace \(\text{Tr}_{\text{reg}}(e^{iTD})\). This is the "Weyl law" for the Connes dilation operator.

1. The Theta Function

The Riemann-Siegel theta function:

\[\theta(T) = \arg\Gamma\left(\frac{1}{4} + \frac{iT}{2}\right) - \frac{T}{2}\log\pi\]

This is the phase in the functional equation of zeta:

\[\zeta(s) = \chi(s) \cdot \zeta(1-s)\]

2. The Derivative and Spectral Density

Theorem (Smooth Part of Trace)

\[\left[\text{Tr}_{\text{reg}}(e^{iTD})\right]_{\text{smooth}} = \Theta'(T) = \frac{1}{2}\log\frac{T}{2\pi} + O\left(\frac{1}{T^2}\right)\]

3. Physical Interpretation

Weyl Law for \(D\):
\(\Theta'(T)\) is the density of the continuous spectrum (after regularization).
The continuous part contributes \(\frac{1}{2}\log(T/2\pi)\) states per unit \(T\).

4. Connection to Counting Function

The smooth counting function:

\[N_{\text{smooth}}(T) = \frac{1}{\pi}\theta(T) + 1\]

Its derivative:

\[\frac{d}{dT}N_{\text{smooth}}(T) = \frac{1}{\pi}\theta'(T)\]

5. The Complete Picture

The full Riemann-von Mangoldt formula:

\[N(T) = N_{\text{smooth}}(T) + S(T) = \frac{1}{\pi}\theta(T) + 1 + S(T)\]

where \(S(T) = O(\log T)\) under RH.

Summary

  • \(\Theta'(T)\) = smooth part of regularized trace
  • Continuous spectrum contributes the "Weyl law"
  • Discrete zeros contribute oscillations \(S(T)\)
  • RH \(\Leftrightarrow S(T) = O(\log T)\)
