Transition Theory: The Physics of Invariant Breaking

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
If physical regimes are fundamentally incompatible, how does the universe evolve from one to another? We introduce Transition Theory, treating the boundary between incompatibility regimes as a physical object governed by its own dynamical laws. We postulate that a Transition $E_{ij}$ is an irreversible map that consumes information to break an invariant. This framework provides the causal mechanism linking the Big Bang (QFT) to the modern universe (GR), identifying the "Arrow of Time" with the accumulation of broken invariants.

The Theory of Regime Incompatibility (TRI) establishes that no static law can span orthogonal regimes. This forces a shift in perspective: from searching for a static "Theory of Everything" to developing a dynamic theory of transitions.

The Transition Operator

We define a transition not as a smooth evolution, but as a discrete operator $E$ acting on the space of laws.

Postulate 1: A physical transition $E_{ij}: R_i \to R_j$ exists if and only if there is a non-zero entropy production $\Delta S > 0$ associated with the loss of symmetry from $R_i$ to $R_j$.
$$ \mathcal{H}_{eff} = \mathcal{H}_i \oplus \mathcal{H}_j + \hat{E}_{int} $$

Unlike standard interaction terms, $\hat{E}_{int}$ is non-Hermitian, representing the irreversible nature of regime change. It does not conserve information.

Classification of Transitions

Transitions are classified by the algebraic structure they destroy:

1. Type I (Commutative): Breaking of abelian symmetries (e.g., Phase transitions in matter). Reversible in principle.

2. Type II (Non-Commutative): Loss of quantum coherence (Decoherence). Effectively irreversible.

3. Type III (Structural): Breaking of spacetime manifold structure (e.g., Singularity dynamics). Fundamentally irreversible.

The Bridge to TDTR

This paper concludes the static description of TRI. By establishing that transitions are physical, entropic processes, we lay the foundation for the Thermodynamic Time Reversal (TDTR) program, which aims to map the complete directed graph of these transitions.

Conclusion

Physics is not a single frozen crystal of laws. It is a cascading flow of breaking symmetries. We do not live in one regime; we live in the debris of the transitions that created it.
