Stage 9: The Forced Geometry Theorem

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We prove that no elliptic operator on any compact Riemannian manifold can have spectral counting function $N(E) \sim E \log E$. Since the Riemann zeros have exactly this counting, any Hilbert-Polya operator must live on a non-Euclidean space: hyperbolic geometry or Connes' noncommutative adele class space.

1. The Theorem

Theorem (Forced Geometry / No-Go for Euclidean RH):

Let $(M, g)$ be a compact $d$-dimensional Riemannian manifold with Laplace-Beltrami operator $\Delta$.

Statement 1 (Weyl's Law):
$$N_M(E) = C_d \cdot \text{Vol}(M) \cdot E^{d/2} + O(E^{(d-1)/2})$$

Statement 2 (Riemann-von Mangoldt):
$$N_{RH}(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi} \log\frac{T}{2\pi} - \frac{T}{2\pi} + O(\log T)$$

Statement 3 (Incompatibility):
For all $d \in \{1, 2, 3, ...\}$: $$\lim_{E \to \infty} \frac{N_{RH}(E)}{E^{d/2}} \neq \text{constant}$$

Corollary: $E \log E \notin \{E^{d/2} : d \in \mathbb{N}\}$

No compact Riemannian manifold of any dimension can have spectral counting $N(E) \sim E \log E$.

2. Numerical Verification

Observation:

  • For $d = 2$: ratio diverges (grows without bound)
  • For $d \geq 3$: ratio vanishes (approaches zero)

There is NO value of $d$ for which the ratio is constant. Therefore $E \log E$ cannot match any Weyl law.

3. What This Eliminates

Eliminated Candidates:
  • Schrodinger operators $-\frac{d^2}{dx^2} + V(x)$ on intervals
  • Laplacians on compact surfaces of any genus
  • Any operator with polynomial Weyl law
  • Berry-Keating type operators $H = xp + V(x)$ on bounded domains
Remaining Candidates:
  • Operators on hyperbolic quotients with cusps
  • Connes' operator on the adele class space $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q} / \mathbb{Q}^*$
  • Berry-Keating regularized on non-compact domains

4. What Produces $E \log E$?

Known sources of $E \log E$ counting:

  1. Non-compact hyperbolic surfaces with cusps
    • Continuous spectrum contribution
    • Eisenstein series and scattering theory
  2. Geodesic length spectrum on hyperbolic surfaces
    • Prime Geodesic Theorem: $\pi(L) \sim e^L / L$
  3. Noncommutative geometry (Connes)
    • The adele class space $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q} / \mathbb{Q}^*$
    • Natural scaling action produces log factors

5. The Logical Chain

[PREMISE 1] Riemann zeros have $N(T) \sim T \log T$

[PREMISE 2] Weyl's law on compact $d$-manifolds gives $N(E) \sim E^{d/2}$

[PREMISE 3] $T \log T \neq E^{d/2}$ for any $d$ (proven above)

[CONCLUSION] The RH operator cannot be a Laplacian on any compact Riemannian manifold

[COROLLARY] The RH operator MUST live on:

  • Hyperbolic space (non-compact), OR
  • Noncommutative space (Connes), OR
  • Some other non-Euclidean structure

This is the Forced Geometry Theorem. It is not a conjecture. It is a mathematical fact.

6. Implications for Hilbert-Polya

The Hilbert-Polya conjecture states:

"The Riemann zeros are eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator"

Our theorem adds:

"...and that operator CANNOT be an elliptic operator on any compact Euclidean manifold"

This is why the Connes program is the leading approach to RH.
