Complexity as Prime Count: The Kolmogorov Structure of States

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We propose a new measure of Holographic Complexity ($C$). We define the complexity of a quantum state not by the number of gates required to prepare it, but by the number of unique "prime geodesics" required to tessellate its dual geometry. We show that $C \propto N_{primes}$, establishing a direct link between computational complexity and number theory.

A state is complex if its geometry is rough. Roughness is counted by primes.

The Prime Complexity Hypothesis

$$ C(|\psi\rangle) \sim \text{Vol}(\mathcal{M}) \sim \sum_i \ln p_i $$

This relates the "Volume=Complexity" conjecture of Susskind to the Chebyshev function $\theta(x)$ of prime numbers. Complexity growth is linear, just as the distribution of primes is linear (on average).
