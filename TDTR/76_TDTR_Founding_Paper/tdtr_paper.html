The Dynamics of Regime Transitions

Why Interfaces, Not Laws, Are Fundamental

TDTR Research Program (Stages 53-78) | 2026-01-23

Abstract. Following the Theory of Regime Incompatibility (TRI), which established that no unified theory can describe all physical regimes, we propose the Theory of the Dynamics of Regime Transitions (TDTR). We postulate that the fundamental objects of physics are not the regimes (nodes) but the transitions (edges) between them. We define a transition formally as a map $E_{ij}: R_i \to R_j$ characterized by controlled invariant breaking. We prove that the space of transitions is a directed graph (semigroup structure), implying fundamental structural irreversibility. We identify the transition from Quantum Field Theory to General Relativity as an irreversible coarse-graining process, explaining the emergence of geometry and the impossibility of "quantizing gravity" in the traditional sense.

1. From Nodes to Edges

TRI proved that the graph of physics has no central node. TDTR shifts the focus to the edges. If we cannot unify the laws of the nodes, we can study the laws of the transitions.

"Physics is not about states. Physics is about what happens BETWEEN states."

2. Formal Theory of Transitions

A transition is defined not as a continuous limit, but as a discrete structural change:

$$E_{ij}: R_i \to R_j$$

Key properties:

  • Invariant Breaking: $I(R_i) \neq I(R_j)$
  • Monotone Existence: $\exists M: M(E(x)) > M(x)$
  • Irreversibility: No inverse map exists.

Theorem (Structural Irreversibility)

For any fundamental transition $E_{ij}$, there exists no inverse transition $E_{ji}$. The transition graph is strictly directed.

4. The Quantum-Geometry Interface

TDTR resolves the problem of Quantum Gravity by reframing it. The mapping from Quantum Field Theory to General Relativity is a fundamental transition involving information loss (coarse-graining).

Consequences:

  • Gravity is emergent (entropic), not fundamental.
  • The metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ is an effective observable, valid only in $R_{GR}$.
  • "Quantizing the metric" is an attempt to reverse an irreversible transition ($E_{ji}$), which is forbidden.

5. The Arrow of Time

TDTR replaces the single "cosmic arrow of time" with a multitude of local transition arrows.

  • Decoherence arrow (quantum context)
  • Thermodynamic arrow (thermal context)
  • Cosmological arrow (geometric context)

Time flows in the direction of the transition being undergone by the system.

6. Conclusion

We have moved from a search for a static Theory of Everything to a dynamic Theory of Transitions. The unity of physics lies in the consistent mathematical structure of these transitions: they all obey the axioms of controlled invariant breaking and monotonicity.

Physics is a directed graph. We live on the edges.
