1. Primes as Orbits

In the adelic space \(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q} / \mathbb{Q}^*\), the dilation flow \(e^{tD}\) has periodic orbits corresponding to primes.

Orbit-Prime Correspondence

  • Prime \(p\) \(\leftrightarrow\) Periodic orbit with length \(\log p\)
  • Weight of contribution: \(\log p / \sqrt{p}\)
  • Higher powers \(p^k\) contribute at \(T = k \log p\)

4. The Formula

Prime Contribution to Trace

\[\text{[Orbital part]} = \sum_p \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{\log p}{p^{k/2}} \cdot \delta(T - k\log p)\]
Key Insight: This is NOT an arbitrary input. It is the UNIQUE form the orbital contribution can have when:
  1. The "lengths" are \(\log p\)
  2. The structure is a trace formula
The primes EMERGE from the geometry of \(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Q}^*\).

5. Comparison with Selberg

Selberg trace formula:

\[\sum_n h(r_n) = \text{(identity)} + \sum_\gamma \sum_k \frac{\ell(\gamma)}{2\sinh(k\ell/2)} \hat{h}(k\ell) + \cdots\]

Weil explicit formula:

\[\sum_\gamma h(\gamma) = \cdots - \sum_p \sum_k \frac{\log p}{p^{k/2}} [\hat{h}(k\log p) + \hat{h}(-k\log p)]\]

These are the SAME formula in different geometric contexts.
