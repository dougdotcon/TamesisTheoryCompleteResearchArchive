Emergent Newton: Deriving $F=ma$ from Entropic Maximization

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We revisit Verlinde's derivation of Newton's Second Law as an entropic force. We clarify the subtle role of the Unruh temperature in this derivation and show that inertia itself is a resistance to entropy production. A particle accelerates not because it is pushed, but because moving maximizes the number of accessible microstates in the Rindler horizon.

Inertia is not a property of mass. It is a property of the vacuum that mass displaces.

The Derivation

$$ F \Delta x = T \Delta S $$

Substituting $T = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}$ (Unruh) and $\Delta S = 2\pi k_B \frac{mc}{\hbar} \Delta x$ (Bekenstein), we obtain:

$$ F = ma $$

Newton is thermodynamic.
