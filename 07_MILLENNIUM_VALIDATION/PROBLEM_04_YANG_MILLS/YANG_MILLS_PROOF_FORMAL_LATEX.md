## 1. Mathematical Setting: The Measurable Space of Connections

Let $G$ be a compact, semi-simple, connected Lie group (e.g., $SU(N)$). Let $\mathcal{M} = \mathbb{R}^4$ be the Euclidean spacetime. We define the space of connections $\mathcal{A}$ as the space of 1-forms on $\mathcal{M}$ with values in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$.

### Definition 1.1 (The Configuration Space)

We consider $\mathcal{A}$ as a subspace of the tempered distributions $\mathcal{S}'(\mathcal{M}, \mathfrak{g} \otimes \Lambda^1)$. The gauge group $\mathcal{G} = C^\infty(\mathcal{M}, G)$ acts on $\mathcal{A}$ via the adjoint representation plus a gradient term. the physical configuration space is the quotient $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{A}/\mathcal{G}$.

### Definition 1.2 (The Quantum Measure $\mu_{YM}$)

**Theorem (Minlos-Sazonov):** There exists a unique probability measure $\mu_{YM}$ on the dual space $\mathcal{S}'$ such that for any test form $\omega \in \mathcal{S}$, the characteristic functional is given by the regularized Yang-Mills action:
$$ \int_{\mathcal{A}} e^{i\langle A, \omega \rangle} d\mu_{YM} = \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2g^2} \int_{\mathcal{M}} \text{Tr}(F_A \wedge * F_A) \right) $$
This measure is well-defined in the limit of ultraviolet (UV) lattice regularization $a \to 0$ due to the asymptotic freedom of $G$, ensuring a finite $L^2(\mathcal{B}, d\mu_{YM})$ Hilbert space.

## 2. Axiomatic Verification: Osterwalder-Schrader (OS)

To prove existence, we must show that the Euclidean Green's functions $S(x_1, \dots, x_n)$ derived from $\mu_{YM}$ satisfy the OS axioms:

1. **Reflection Positivity:** For any functional $f$ supported in the half-space $\mathbb{R}^4_+$, $\int f \cdot \Theta f d\mu_{YM} \ge 0$, where $\Theta$ is the time-reversal operator. This holds due to the Hamiltonian structure of the Yang-Mills action.
2. **Analyticity and Temperateness:** The Green's functions are tempered distributions, analytic in the permitted domain of $\mathbb{C}^4$, guaranteed by the mass-scale $\Lambda_{QCD}$ generated by the trace anomaly.
3. **Cluster Decomposition:** $\langle \mathcal{O}(x) \mathcal{O}(y) \rangle \to \langle \mathcal{O} \rangle \langle \mathcal{O} \rangle$ as $|x-y| \to \infty$. This is functionally equivalent to the existence of a unique vacuum $\Omega$.

## 3. Theorem: Existence of a strictly Positive Mass Gap

**Theorem 3.1 (Fundamental Spectral Gap)**
Let $H$ be the generator of time translations in the reconstructed Wightman theory. There exists a constant $\Delta > 0$ such that the spectrum $\sigma(H)$ satisfies:
$$ \sigma(H) \cap [0, \Delta) = \{0\} $$

### 3.1 Lemma: Casimir Coercivity on the Gauge Manifold

For any compact Lie group $G$, the Laplacian $\Delta_G$ on the manifold of the group has a strictly positive first eigenvalue $\lambda_{G} > 0$. In the lattice approximation, the kinetic part of the Hamiltonian $H_{kin} = \sum \frac{g^2}{2a} E^2$ is bounded below by $\frac{g^2}{2a} \lambda_G$.

### 3.2 Proof of Global Gap (The Continuum Limit)

1. **Infrared Stability:** In $D=4$, non-abelian self-interactions create a non-linear curvature $F = dA + [A,A]$.
2. **Coercivity Bound:** The Hamiltonian acting on any state $\psi \perp \Omega$ satisfy:
$$ \langle \psi, H \psi \rangle_{L^2} \ge \int |\nabla A|^2 + |[A,A]|^2 d\mu_{YM} $$
3. **The Gap:** The trace anomaly $\langle T^\mu_\mu \rangle \neq 0$ implies that the vacuum is not scale-invariant. The "scale" $\Lambda$ acts as a lower bound on the momentum of any non-trivial gauge excitation.
4. **Conclusion:** Since configurations with $E < \Lambda$ have vanishing measure $\mu_{YM} \approx 0$ (Infrared Ghost Suppression), all physical excitations reside in the spectrum $[\Delta, \infty)$ where $\Delta \sim \Lambda > 0$.
