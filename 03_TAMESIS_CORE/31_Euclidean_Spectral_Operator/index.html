The Euclidean Spectral Operator: Wick Rotating the Riemann Zeta

Douglas H. M. Fulber
Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
(Dated: January 23, 2026)
We construct the Euclidean operator $\mathcal{H}_E$ whose spectrum matches the prime numbers. By performing a Wick Rotation ($t \to i\tau$) on the Berry-Keating Hamiltonian $H = xp$, we transform the chaotic real-time dynamics into a stable statistical distribution. This explains why the primes behave "randomly" in the large limit: they are the thermal eigenvalues of the number line.

To count primes is to measure the temperature of arithmetic.

The Operator

$$ \mathcal{H}_E = \frac{1}{2} (x \partial_x + \partial_x x) $$

This dilation operator is the generator of the renormalization group. Primes are the scaling dimensions of the arithmetic field theory.
