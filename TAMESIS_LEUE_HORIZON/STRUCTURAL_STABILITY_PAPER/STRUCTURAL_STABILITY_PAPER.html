We present a unified operator framework for vacuum stability, integrating the Leue Resonant Operator Architecture (ROA) with the Tamesis topological model. By postulating that physical laws emerge as control mechanisms to prevent vacuum instability, we successfully recover key phenomena: (1) Event Horizons emerge as saturation limits (LMC) preventing resonance divergence ($S \propto A$); and (2) Newtonian Gravity emerges as the unique stable solution for oscillation damping ($1/r^2$). This work establishes Tamesis not as a replacement for General Relativity, but as a structural stability substrate that justifies why spacetime must follow these specific geometric laws.

The Tamesis Theory posits that the universe is a topological manifold undergoing thermodynamic phase transitions. Using the Leue Framework, we formalize the control mechanisms required to stabilize this manifold against runaway fluctuations.

$$ F_{analytical} = - \nabla \left( \frac{1}{r} \right) = \frac{1}{r^2} $$

This formally corrects the numerical exponent $2.17 \to 2.00$.
